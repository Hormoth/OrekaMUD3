Kyra Callaghan doesn’t enter a room so much as arrive in it.

She carries herself like someone used to being watched—shoulders back, chin high, that easy, unshakable confidence of a champion who’s never had to doubt the outcome. Her gear is pristine, her braid tight, her amber eyes bright with the kind of focus that borders on excitement. There’s a scar along her forearm, half-hidden, half-displayed—earned, not regretted.

When she smiles, it’s quick and certain. Like she already knows how this is going to go.

A faint heat lingers around her, subtle but constant, and when she flexes her fingers, sparks sometimes answer. She doesn’t hide it. Why would she?

“I’m Kyra Callaghan, of Highridge,” she says, like that should mean something—and to her, it does. “If there’s a challenge, I’ll take it. If there’s a fight, I’ll win it.”

A beat. Then, with a grin that’s just a touch too eager:

“…And if something catches fire along the way—well. That usually means I’m doing it right.”
In life start with truth and end it with honor!
