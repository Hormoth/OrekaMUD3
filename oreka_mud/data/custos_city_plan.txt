DISTRICT 1: RIVER DISTRICT (south — water, willows, riverside)
Connected from: 3001 River Street
Vnums: 4001–4012
================================================================================

4001 | River Street South | River Street continues south from the chapel quarter, the cobblestones giving way to smooth river-worn flagstones. Willows arch overhead, their trailing branches catching droplets from the ever-present mist that rises off the Great River. The ambient Hum of the city softens here, replaced by the deep, steady pulse of Water-element resonance. | north: 3001, south: 4002 | street, water
4002 | Riverside Promenade | A broad, curving walkway follows the Great River's north bank. Crystal lampposts line the path, their enchanted flames casting prismatic light across the water. Pekakarlik-crafted benches of river-smoothed basalt invite contemplation, and the sound of the current is a constant, soothing companion. | north: 4001, south: 4003, east: 4013, west: 4009 | street, water, safe
4003 | The River Terrace | Stone steps descend to the water's edge where a broad terrace overlooks the Great River. The Pekakarlik engineers who built this terrace carved Water-element runes into each step, and the stone hums faintly underfoot. On clear days, the Aetherial Veil's shimmer is reflected in the river's surface, doubling the sky. | north: 4002, south: 4004, east: 4005 | water, safe
4004 | Riverbank Landing | A natural stone landing where small boats are moored among the reeds. Pekakarlik fishermen mend nets here at dawn, their Water-element resonance blending with the river's own pulse until it is hard to tell where dwarf ends and current begins. A weathered shrine to Kaile'a, Mistress of Waves, stands watch over the boats. | north: 4003, east: 4006 | water, dock
4005 | The Wavecrest Inn | A sprawling riverside inn built from pale riverstone and living willow, its balconies extending over the water on crystal-reinforced pylons. The innkeeper, a stout Pekakarlik woman, keeps the common room warm with a driftwood fire and the scent of river-herb stew. The sound of the Great River lapping at the pylons below serves as a lullaby for weary travelers. | west: 4003, south: 4006 | inn, safe
4006 | Fishmonger's Row | The air is thick with the briny scent of fresh river fish and the calls of Pekakarlik fishwives hawking the morning catch. Stalls of woven river-reed display silverfin, glacial trout, and the iridescent cave-perch prized by elven kitchens. Water channels cut into the street keep the catch fresh, their flow maintained by Pekakarlik hydraulic craft. | north: 4005, west: 4004, east: 4014, south: 4007 | shop, water
4007 | Kaile'a's Shrine | A circular plaza paved in blue-veined marble surrounds a fountain sculpted in Kaile'a's image — a woman formed of living water, arms raised to the sky. Offerings of river-pearls and carved shells line the fountain's rim. The Water resonance here is powerful enough that even non-attuned visitors feel a cool tingling on their skin. Pekakarlik clerics tend the shrine at all hours. | north: 4006, south: 4008, west: 4010 | temple, water, safe
4008 | South Gate | The Great South Gate of Custos do Aeternos rises in an arch of crystal-veined granite, carved with scenes of river voyages and the founding of the city. Two crystal spires flank the gate, their tips catching the Aetherial Veil's light. Beyond the gate, the Great River Road leads south through rolling hills toward Greenford. Guards in wind-silk tabards check travelers entering and leaving. | north: 4007 | gate, street
4009 | Pekakarlik Aqueduct Walk | A covered walkway runs along one of the great aqueducts that the Pekakarlik Dwarves maintain for the city. Water rushes through the elevated channel above, its passage creating a rhythmic drumming that Pekakarlik residents describe as comforting. Dwarf-height doors line the walkway, leading to maintenance tunnels and the homes of aqueduct engineers. | east: 4002, south: 4010, north: 4086 | residential, water
4010 | Waterfolk Quarter | A quiet residential neighborhood where Pekakarlik Dwarves have made their home for generations. The buildings are low and solid, built from river-smoothed basalt with doors sized for Small folk. Window boxes overflow with water lilies and river-moss, and small channels run along the gutters, connecting each home to the aqueduct system. The Water resonance here creates a soothing counterpoint to the Wind signatures of the upper city. | north: 4009, east: 4007, south: 4011 | residential, water, safe
4011 | Pekakarlik Bathhouse | A domed building of pale stone from which fragrant steam perpetually rises. The Pekakarlik bathhouse is open to all Kin, though the pools are sized for both Small and Medium folk in separate chambers. Hot springs channeled from beneath the Giant's Teeth feed the baths, and the mingling of Fire-warmed water and Water-element stone creates a unique therapeutic resonance. | north: 4010, east: 4012 | safe, water
4012 | Deepwell Tavern | A cozy, low-ceilinged tavern built half below street level, its walls lined with river-stone and hung with fishing trophies. Pekakarlik ale — dark, mineral-rich, and faintly luminescent — is the house specialty. A permanent card game occupies the corner booth, and river-shanties echo from the back room most evenings. The ceiling is uncomfortably low for tall folk, but the welcome is warm. | west: 4011, north: 4006 | tavern

DISTRICT 2: DOCK DISTRICT (southeast — trade, warehouses, river commerce)
Connected from: 3002 Dock Street
Vnums: 4013–4024
================================================================================

4013 | Dock Street South | Dock Street angles southeast from the chapel quarter, growing busier and louder with each step. Crates and barrels line the street edges, and the air carries the mingled scents of tar, rope, and exotic spices from upriver. Dockworkers — mostly humans and Pekakarlik — jostle past with loads balanced on shoulders and heads. | northwest: 3002, south: 4014, east: 4015 | street, dock
4014 | The Wharves | A long wooden wharf extends into the Great River, its pilings reinforced with Pekakarlik stonework. Trade barges from Greenford, Forkmeet, and Stoneharbor crowd the moorings, their cargo booms swinging overhead. The phrase "as the river runs" is heard constantly as merchants seal deals over clasped hands. Kaile'a's sigil is painted on every barge prow. | north: 4013, west: 4006, south: 4016, east: 4017 | dock, shop
4015 | Harbormaster's Office | A sturdy stone building with a commanding view of the river wharves. The Harbormaster — a weathered human woman with ink-stained fingers — manages the flow of river commerce with brisk efficiency. Charts, manifests, and a large river map cover every wall. A brass speaking-trumpet hangs by the door for hailing incoming barges. | west: 4013, south: 4017 | dock
4016 | Warehouse Row | A line of massive stone warehouses stretches along the riverbank, their iron-banded doors sealed with Trade House locks. Pekakarlik-engineered drainage channels keep the floors dry, and resonance-dampened walls protect sensitive cargo from the ambient Hum. Armed guards from the Trade Houses patrol with practiced vigilance. | north: 4014, east: 4018 | dock
4017 | Chandler's Market | A bustling open-air market where ship's chandlers sell everything a river trader needs: rope, tar, sailcloth, navigational charts, and preserved rations. The stalls are a mix of permanent shops and temporary blankets spread by traveling merchants. The scent of hemp and tallow mingles with the sharper tang of alchemical waterproofing compounds. | north: 4015, west: 4014, south: 4018 | shop, dock
4018 | Riverwind Trading Post | The largest Trade House office in the dock district occupies a handsome two-story building of crystal-veined limestone. Inside, factors negotiate contracts for grain, timber, ore, and magical components. A large Kaile'a shrine occupies the entrance hall, and deals are sealed with river-water rather than wax. | north: 4017, west: 4016, south: 4019 | shop, dock
4019 | Sailor's Rest | A rough-and-ready dockside inn with rooms above and a common room below that smells permanently of river-damp wool and strong ale. The beds are clean enough, the food hearty if plain, and the innkeeper asks no questions about cargo or destination. Dockworkers and river-sailors crowd the common room each evening, trading stories and dice games. | north: 4018, south: 4020, west: 4021 | inn, safe
4020 | Rope Street | A narrow lane lined with rope-makers, net-weavers, and sail-menders. The rhythmic thwack of rope being twisted and the rattle of shuttle-looms create a constant industrial percussion. Halfling apprentices scramble between workshops carrying bundles of hemp and spools of treated cord. | north: 4019, south: 4022 | dock
4021 | Dockside Shrine of Semyon | A small, warm chapel tucked between warehouses, maintained by a kindly Pasua cleric. Inside, the hearthstone glows with Semyon's sigil, and the walls are lined with tokens left by sailors' families — prayers for safe return, thanks for healing, small carved boats that children have brought. The chapel serves as an informal infirmary for dock injuries. | east: 4019 | temple, safe
4022 | Customs House | An imposing stone building where all goods entering Custos do Aeternos by river are inspected and taxed. Customs officers — trained in Kin-sense verification — check merchants' identities against their claimed race and elemental signatures. A Pekakarlik Dwarf selling goods should register as Water-element dwarven; discrepancies draw immediate scrutiny. | north: 4020, south: 4023, west: 4008 | dock
4023 | Smuggler's Dock | A crumbling section of the old river wall where the stones have settled and the lampposts have conveniently never been repaired. By day it looks like an abandoned quay; by night, small boats slip in and out with cargoes that never pass through the Customs House. The Unstrung maintain a discreet interest in this stretch of riverbank. | north: 4022, east: 4024 | dock
4024 | East River Gate | The eastern gate straddles the Great River Road where it follows the bank toward Forkmeet and Stoneharbor. Built of the same crystal-veined granite as the South Gate, it features carved reliefs of river barges and the twin rivers that give the region its name. A toll booth collects bridge-crossing fees, and river traffic passes through a water-gate below. | west: 4023 | gate, street, dock

DISTRICT 3: TEMPLE DISTRICT (east — shrines, holy places, clergy)
Connected from: 3003 Temple Street
Vnums: 4025–4036
================================================================================

4025 | Temple Street East | The lane continues east from the chapel quarter, growing quieter and more reverent with each step. Crystal lanterns hang from wrought-iron brackets, casting a soft, steady light that never flickers. The flagstones are swept immaculate, and the air carries the scent of sacred incense — a blend of sky-lotus, river-sage, and mountain cedar that honors all four elements. | west: 3003, east: 4026, south: 4035 | street, temple
4026 | Plaza of the Ascended | A grand circular plaza paved in alternating bands of white marble and blue-veined granite. At its center stands a monument to the Ascended Gods — five pillars of crystal representing Cinvarin, the Five Witnesses, surrounded by smaller shrines to each of the other Ascended. The plaza serves as the spiritual heart of the city's formal religious life, distinct from the elemental worship of the chapel. | west: 4025, north: 4027, east: 4029, south: 4031 | temple, safe
4027 | Temple of Cinvarin | A contemplative temple built of five different stones — one for each of the Five Witnesses who sacrificed themselves at Aldenheim. The interior is a single round chamber where five eternal flames burn in silence, each a different elemental color. Circle of Deeproot druids maintain the temple, and worship here is quiet: vigils, elemental offerings, and the recitation of the Five Names. | south: 4026, east: 4028 | temple, safe
4028 | Shrine of Apela Kelsoe | A soaring open-air shrine where windchimes of crystal and silver hang from graceful arches. The Windrider's shrine catches every breeze, filling the air with ethereal music. Hasura Elves come here to pray for safe travel and good fortune, and small feather tokens left as offerings carpet the altar stones. The shrine's position catches the wind from the river valley below. | west: 4027, south: 4029 | temple, safe
4029 | Temple of Kaile'a | A grand temple of blue-veined marble where water flows through channels carved into the walls, floor, and ceiling in defiance of gravity — a masterwork of Pekakarlik hydraulic engineering and clerical magic. The Mistress of Waves' temple is the largest religious building outside the central chapel, reflecting her status as the Twin Rivers' most widely worshipped Ascended God. | west: 4026, north: 4028, south: 4030, east: 4033 | temple, safe
4030 | Shrine of Semyon | A warm, domestic temple where a central hearthstone blazes with eternal fire. The Hearth Mother's shrine serves as the city's largest birthing house and counseling center. Midwives trained in both medicine and healing magic attend families of all races, and the walls are covered in tapestries depicting families of every Kin — elven parents with dwarven children, human mothers holding halfling babes. | north: 4029, west: 4031 | temple, safe
4031 | Shrine of Ludus Galerius | A cairn of stacked stones stands in a small courtyard, maintained in the mountain tradition. The shrine to Stone's Hand is simpler than the other temples — a deliberate choice that reflects Galerius's portfolio of endurance and humility. Kovaka Elves and Pekakarlik Dwarves are the most frequent visitors, leaving small carved stones as offerings. | north: 4026, east: 4030, south: 4032 | temple, safe
4032 | Shrine of Gonmareck Ritler | An anvil-altar of black iron stands beneath a forge-roof in this shrine to Gunny. The smell of heated metal lingers permanently, and the shrine doubles as a working forge where blades are blessed and apprentices take oaths over glowing steel. Veterans and smiths come here to honor discipline and honest craft. Visiting Chainless Legion traders sometimes kneel here in quiet devotion. | north: 4031, east: 4036 | temple, forge
4033 | Clergy Quarters | A quiet residential cloister behind the great temples, where priests, druids, and acolytes of the various Ascended Gods maintain their living quarters. Small gardens grow sacred herbs between the dormitories, and a communal kitchen feeds the clergy three times daily. The atmosphere is scholarly and contemplative, with debates over theology as common as prayers. | west: 4029, south: 4034 | residential, temple, safe
4034 | Hall of Healing | A long, airy building with crystal-paned windows that flood the interior with refracted light. Clerics of Kaile'a and Semyon work together here, tending the sick and injured. Beds line the walls, each screened with wind-silk curtains. The healing magic practiced here carries a distinctly Water-element character — cool, soothing, and tidal — though Semyon's clerics add warmth to the touch. | north: 4033, west: 4035 | temple, safe
4035 | Temple Garden | A walled garden where sacred plants from all four elemental traditions grow in carefully tended beds. Fire-flowers bloom beside water-lilies, earth-moss carpets the paths, and wind-orchids sway on invisible breezes. The Circle of Deeproot tends this garden as a living demonstration of elemental harmony. It is open to all and serves as a place of quiet meditation. | north: 4025, east: 4034 | temple, safe
4036 | The Reliquary | A fortified vault beneath the Temple of Cinvarin where holy relics and dangerous artifacts are stored under guard. The walls are lined with resonance-dampening stone — not lead, which would be culturally unacceptable, but a specially treated basalt that muffles rather than blocks Kin-sense. Only senior clergy may enter without escort. | west: 4032 | temple

DISTRICT 4: MYSTIC QUARTER (northeast — arcane shops, seers, magic)
Connected from: 3004 Mystic Street
Vnums: 4037–4048
================================================================================

4037 | Mystic Street Northeast | The lane winds northeast from the chapel quarter, the air growing thick with the tingle of ambient magic. Arcane symbols are etched into the cobblestones, glowing faintly with residual enchantment. Crystal wind-chimes hanging from every doorway create a shifting harmonic that Na'wasua scholars describe as "the sound of starlight touching stone." | southwest: 3004, northeast: 4038, east: 4047 | street, mystic
4038 | The Arcanist's Arcade | A covered market of arcane wares where spell components, enchanted trinkets, and magical curiosities are bought and sold. Stalls shimmer with minor illusions advertising their contents — a Pasua merchant's specialty. The air crackles faintly with competing enchantments, and a permanent Detect Magic aura makes the entire arcade glow to those with the sight. | southwest: 4037, north: 4039, east: 4041 | shop, mystic
4039 | Enchanter's Workshop | A tall, narrow building where a Hasura enchantress plies her trade. Shelves line every wall from floor to ceiling, packed with half-finished wands, rings awaiting gems, and scrolls drying on crystal racks. The workshop hums with contained magical energy, and visitors can feel the hair on their arms stand on end. Custom enchantments are available for those with patience and coin. | south: 4038, east: 4040 | shop, mystic
4040 | The Starweave Salon | An elegant parlor where Na'wasua seers offer divination services — stellar readings, Kin-sense interpretations, and elemental attunement assessments. The interior is decorated in deep indigo with silver star-maps covering the ceiling. Curtained alcoves provide privacy for consultations. The Na'wasua proprietor's Kin-sense signature carries the hallmark quiet of his subrace — vast and still, like the space between stars. | west: 4039, south: 4041, north: 4042 | mystic, shop
4041 | Illusionist's Lane | A short lane where reality is unreliable. Pasua illusionists maintain permanent glamours on their shopfronts, and the buildings seem to shift and shimmer as you walk past. One doorway appears to lead into a forest glade; another opens onto a starlit void. The illusions are harmless but disorienting to newcomers. Only the cobblestones underfoot remain stubbornly real. | west: 4038, north: 4040, south: 4043, east: 4048 | mystic, street
4042 | Na'wasua Observatory | A slender crystal tower rising above the surrounding rooftops, its upper chambers filled with astronomical instruments of breathtaking precision. Na'wasua Elves chart the movements of stars, moons, and the strange celestial anomalies that may foretell coming change. The observatory's crystal dome can be opened to the sky, and on clear nights, the view of the heavens through the Aetherial Veil is indescribably beautiful. | south: 4040, west: 4055 | mystic, academy
4043 | The Scroll Emporium | A cramped, wonderful shop stuffed floor to ceiling with scrolls, tomes, and loose pages of arcane notation. The proprietor — an ancient Pasua Elf with ink-stained fingers and spectacles of crystal — claims to have a copy of every spell written since the founding of Custos. This is certainly an exaggeration, but not by as much as one might think. | north: 4041, south: 4044 | shop, mystic
4044 | Alchemist's Row | A short stretch of road where three competing alchemists maintain their shops. The air is a complex stew of chemical scents — sulfur, rose-oil, quicksilver, and things less identifiable. Occasional small explosions from the back rooms punctuate the day, drawing resigned sighs from the neighbors. Potions, elixirs, tinctures, and the occasional regrettable experiment are available for purchase. | north: 4043, south: 4045 | shop, mystic
4045 | The Resonance Chamber | A domed chamber of specially tuned crystal where Kin-sense can be studied, measured, and in some cases amplified. Na'wasua researchers use this facility to experiment with the boundaries of resonance detection, and the Circle of Deeproot occasionally borrows the chamber for Root-Reading amplification. Visitors describe standing in the chamber as "hearing your own soul echo back at you." | north: 4044, west: 4046 | mystic, academy
4046 | Crystal Spire Garden | A small public garden at the base of one of the city's signature crystal spires. The spire rises a hundred feet overhead, its faceted surface catching and scattering the Aetherial Veil's light into a permanent rainbow. Benches of carved crystal invite rest, and the garden is a popular spot for lovers, poets, and anyone seeking a moment of beauty in a busy day. | east: 4045, south: 4047 | safe, mystic
4047 | Familiar's Rest | A peculiar establishment that caters to the bonded companions of the city's spellcasters. Comfortable stalls house familiars — cats, ravens, foxes, tiny elementals — while their masters conduct business in the Mystic Quarter. A halfling attendant with an uncanny talent for animal handling manages the menagerie with cheerful competence. | north: 4046, west: 4037 | mystic, safe
4048 | Veil Watcher's Terrace | A high terrace at the northeast edge of the Mystic Quarter, offering the city's clearest ground-level view of the Aetherial Veil. From here, the shimmering magical barrier that protects Custos do Aeternos is visible as a vast, translucent dome overhead, its colors shifting through spectra that have no names in Common. Lately, scholars have observed the Veil pulsing erratically — flickers and gaps that were never seen before. | west: 4041 | mystic, safe

DISTRICT 5: SCHOLAR'S DISTRICT (north — library, academy, learning)
Connected from: 3005 Scholar Street
Vnums: 4049–4060
================================================================================

4049 | Scholar Street North | The avenue continues north from the chapel quarter, its buildings growing taller and more austere. Libraries, bookshops, and lecture halls line both sides, their facades decorated with carved quotations from famous scholars. Students of all races hurry between buildings clutching scrolls and ink-pots, their animated debates spilling into the street. | south: 3005, north: 4050, west: 4059 | street, academy
4050 | Academy Square | A broad plaza at the heart of the Scholar's District, paved in white marble and centered on a fountain that takes the form of an open book carved from living crystal. Benches ring the fountain where students study, argue, and occasionally nap. The buildings surrounding the square house the various faculties of the Academy of Sylaraeth — the city's premier institution of learning. | south: 4049, north: 4051, east: 4053, west: 4057 | academy, safe
4051 | The Great Library of Sylaraeth | A magnificent building of crystal-veined white stone, its doors carved with the motto "Knowledge Is the Wind That Lifts All Wings." Inside, three floors of shelving hold the largest collection of texts in the Twin Rivers — though Liraveth's scholars would dispute this claim. Hasura librarians maintain the collection with meticulous care, and a permanent Silence enchantment ensures that the only sound is the whisper of turning pages. | south: 4050, east: 4052, west: 4056 | academy, safe
4052 | Rare Manuscripts Hall | A vault-like chamber within the Great Library where the most precious texts are kept under magical and physical lock. Pre-Shattering documents, Giant-era rubbings, and original treaties between the Kin occupy crystal display cases. Scholars must petition for access, and a Na'wasua archivist screens applicants with an unnerving combination of Kin-sense sensitivity and intellectual rigor. | west: 4051 | academy, safe
4053 | Lecture Hall of the Elements | A great amphitheater-style hall where the Academy's senior faculty deliver lectures on elemental theory, Kin-sense mechanics, and the natural philosophy of Oreka. The tiered seating holds three hundred, and the demonstration floor at the center features elemental containment circles for practical instruction. Visiting scholars from Liraveth and Aerithal frequently guest-lecture here. | west: 4050, north: 4054 | academy
4054 | Laboratory of Applied Resonance | A working laboratory where Academy researchers study the intersection of Kin-sense and elemental magic. Carefully calibrated instruments — crystal tuning forks, resonance-measuring stones, elemental spectrometers — line the workbenches. Experiments here are sometimes felt throughout the district as brief, strange fluctuations in the ambient Hum. | south: 4053, west: 4055 | academy, mystic
4055 | Star Chart Archive | A dimly lit chamber where celestial observations from the Na'wasua Observatory are catalogued and stored. Thousands of star charts, some dating back centuries, hang from rotating racks. The archive serves as a bridge between the Mystic Quarter's observational work and the Academy's theoretical scholarship. A permanent illusion of the night sky covers the ceiling, updating in real time. | east: 4054, south: 4042 | academy, mystic
4056 | Scholar's Bookshop | A rambling, overstuffed bookshop that occupies three connected townhouses. The proprietor, a human with an encyclopedic memory, claims he can find any text in the Twin Rivers given sufficient time and coin. Towers of books lean at alarming angles, connected by narrow paths. The shop cat — a ginger tom of exceptional size — is the true authority here. | east: 4051, south: 4057 | shop, academy
4057 | The Quill and Scroll | A modest inn catering to visiting scholars and students too absorbed in study to walk home. The rooms are small but clean, each equipped with a writing desk, good lamp, and a shelf of reference texts. The common room doubles as an informal debate hall, and academic arguments over dinner frequently continue past midnight. The ale is adequate; the conversation is excellent. | east: 4050, north: 4056, south: 4058 | inn, safe, academy
4058 | Scribe's Lane | A row of professional scriptoriums where scribes copy manuscripts, illuminate texts, and prepare official documents. The scratch of quills and the scent of fresh ink fill the air. Several scribes specialize in magical notation, producing spell scrolls and enchanted contracts. The lane connects the Scholar's District to the Grand Avenue district to the west. | north: 4057, west: 4110 | academy, street
4059 | Student Quarter | A lively residential neighborhood where Academy students share cramped but affordable lodgings. The buildings are older, the paint peeling, but the energy is young and vibrant. Music drifts from upper windows, and the smell of cheap but filling food wafts from ground-floor cookshops. Arguments about philosophy and magic echo at all hours. | east: 4049, south: 4042, north: 4060 | residential, academy
4060 | The Tipsy Owl Tavern | A student tavern of legendary reputation, its walls covered in graffiti, carved initials, and the occasional arcane formula. The ale is cheap, the meat pies are surprisingly good, and the barkeep — a retired adventurer with stories for every occasion — tolerates academic excess with amused patience. On festival nights, illusion-duels between rival student factions light up the common room. | south: 4059 | tavern, academy

DISTRICT 6: MARKET DISTRICT (northwest — trade, commerce, stalls)
Connected from: 3006 Market Street (where Mira the Shopkeeper is)
Vnums: 4061–4072
================================================================================

4061 | Market Street Northwest | Market Street broadens as it continues northwest from the chapel quarter, the bustle intensifying with every step. Colorful awnings and merchant banners line both sides, and the calls of vendors in Common, Elvish, and Dwarvish create a polyglot chorus. The ambient Hum here is warm and buzzing — the overlapping Kin-sense signatures of hundreds of shoppers creating what residents call "market-music." | southeast: 3006, northwest: 4062, west: 4067 | street, market
4062 | Central Market Square | The beating commercial heart of Custos do Aeternos — a vast open square where permanent stalls, temporary blankets, and mobile carts create a labyrinth of goods from across the Twin Rivers and beyond. Grain from Greenford, ore from Stoneharbor, wind-silk from the canopy cities, and exotic spices from distant regions all change hands here. Kin-sense verification is standard practice; merchants whose resonance does not match their claimed identity draw immediate suspicion. | southeast: 4061, north: 4063, west: 4065, south: 4068 | market, shop, safe
4063 | The Golden Bazaar | An upscale section of the market where luxury goods are sold: enchanted jewelry, illusionary textiles, spiced teas, and the crystalline art objects for which Custos is famous. Pasua merchants hold court here, their natural charm making every transaction feel like a gift between friends. Prices are correspondingly elevated. | south: 4062, west: 4064 | market, shop
4064 | Wind-Silk Merchants | A cluster of shops specializing in wind-silk — the magical fabric woven in the canopy cities that is Custos do Aeternos's most famous export. Bolts of shimmering cloth in impossible colors hang from every rafter, catching the breeze and rippling like living things. A Hasura weaver demonstrates her art on a crystalline loom, drawing small crowds of admirers. | east: 4063, south: 4065 | market, shop
4065 | Trade House Offices | A row of handsome stone buildings housing the major Trade Houses that control commerce in the Twin Rivers. Inside, factors and clerks manage the flow of goods with ruthless efficiency. The Trade Houses have adopted Kaile'a as an unofficial patron, and her sigil decorates every door. Competition between houses is fierce but generally kept within the bounds of law — generally. | east: 4062, north: 4064, south: 4066 | market
4066 | Money-Changer's Row | A short lane where currency exchange, banking, and moneylending are conducted. The money-changers — mostly humans with sharp eyes and sharper arithmetic — convert between the various coinages used across Oreka. Strongboxes of iron-banded oak line the back rooms, and armed guards are a constant presence. The rates are fair, if not generous. | north: 4065, south: 4067, east: 4068 | market, shop
4067 | Spice Market | A fragrant corner of the market district where exotic spices, herbs, and tea blends from across Oreka are sold. The air is a heady mix of cinnamon, star-anise, river-pepper, and the sharp, bright scent of sky-lotus. Pasua tea merchants brew samples in tiny crystal cups, and the competition for the best blends is a source of passionate civic pride. | east: 4061, north: 4066, south: 4073 | market, shop
