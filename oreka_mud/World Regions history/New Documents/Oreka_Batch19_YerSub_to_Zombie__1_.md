# YER-SUB

The steppe splits. Not violently — there is no earthquake, no crack of breaking stone — but a seam appears in the earth as if the ground were opening a door. From within rises a figure of compressed soil, clay, and living stone: humanoid in outline but faceless, broad as a wall, and rooted to the earth by tendrils of bedrock that trail from its legs like a gown. It smells of rain-wet soil and deep stone. When it moves, the steppe moves with it — grass leans toward it, stones shift, and for a radius of a hundred feet, the ground feels *aware*.

The yer-sub are earth-spirits of the Eternal Steppe — elemental guardians of the deep soil, the aquifer layer, and the stone-root that lies beneath the grassland. Mytroan wind-callers know them as *yer-ana* — "earth-mothers" — and consider their appearance the most profound sign of the Lord of Stone's attention.

### Combat

A yer-sub is a territorial defender, not an aggressor — it manifests when the steppe's deep-earth is threatened (mining, tunnelling, Dómnathar subterranean expansion, or corruption of aquifers) and fights to expel the threat. Earth command reshapes the battlefield; steppe ward creates a zone of accelerated growth and healing around the yer-sub that benefits allies and the land itself.

Against underground intruders (Dómnathar tunnellers, bulettes, ankhegs), a yer-sub earth-glides to intercept and uses earth mastery (+1 attack/damage when both it and foe touch ground) to devastating effect. Against surface threats, it relies on slam attacks and earth command to isolate and overwhelm.

### Special Abilities

**Earth Glide (Ex):** As earth elemental — can pass through stone and earth as easily as swimming through water.

**Earth Mastery (Ex):** +1 on attack and damage rolls when both the yer-sub and its opponent touch the ground.

**Earth Command (Su):** 3/day, a yer-sub can reshape terrain in a 60-foot radius — raising stone walls (up to 10 ft. high, 1 ft. thick, 20 ft. long), creating difficult terrain (churned earth), or opening/closing fissures (Reflex DC 20 to avoid falling into a 10-ft.-deep pit). The reshaping takes a full-round action and is permanent.

**Steppe Ward (Su):** A yer-sub can establish a zone of earth-blessing in a 100-foot radius (full-round action, maintained by concentration). Within this zone, natural plants grow at ten times normal speed, natural water sources are purified, and all creatures with an earth elemental affinity gain fast healing 2. The zone persists for 1 hour after the yer-sub stops concentrating.

**Deep-Soil Guardian Resonance (Su):** A yer-sub registers on Kin-sense as the Lord of Stone's nurturing aspect — the deep soil that grows all things, the bedrock that supports all structures. Detectable at 120 feet. The resonance is experienced as stability — Kin within range feel grounded, calm, and certain of the earth beneath their feet. Mytroan wind-callers describe it as "the steppe remembering it has bones."

### Society

Yer-sub are the Eternal Steppe's hidden guardians — earth elementals that exist within the bedrock layer and manifest only when the land requires defence or when summoned through Mytroan earth-rituals.

Mytroan culture has the deepest relationship with yer-sub of any Kin people. Mytroan earth-singers (a specialisation within the wind-caller tradition, focused on the Lord of Stone rather than the Youngest Brother) maintain ritual sites at known yer-sub manifestation-points — natural springs, exposed bedrock formations, and seasonal camp-sites where the steppe's stone-root is closest to the surface. Earth-singers can petition a yer-sub for manifestation through a daylong ritual involving poured offerings (milk, fermented mare's blood, crushed stone) and sustained chanting.

The relationship is not servile. Yer-sub are not summoned — they are *asked*, and they may refuse. A yer-sub that manifests in response to a petition will evaluate the request, and if it judges the threat to the steppe genuine, it will act. If it judges the request trivial or selfish, it returns to the deep-earth without comment.

Yer-sub are natural enemies of Dómnathar subterranean expansion. The Dómnathar tunnelling-networks that extend beneath the Eternal Steppe are periodically disrupted by yer-sub intervention — collapsed tunnels, sealed passages, and the occasional destroyed digging-team.

### Lore

**DC 17 (Knowledge [the planes]):** Yer-sub are Large earth elementals that guard the Eternal Steppe's bedrock and aquifer systems. They manifest at geological sites of significance and fight to protect the deep-earth. DR 10/—, earth glide, tremorsense 120 ft.

**DC 22:** Yer-sub can reshape terrain through earth command and establish healing steppe wards. Mytroan earth-singers petition them through daylong rituals, but yer-sub may refuse if the threat is not genuine. They are natural enemies of Dómnathar subterranean expansion.

**DC 27:** The oldest yer-sub (Huge, 16+ HD) incorporate fossil-stone and aquifer-crystal into their forms and may have existed since before the Giants. Their steppe ward can heal a blighted area over weeks of sustained presence.

# YUKI-ONNA

The snow falls harder after midnight, and the trail disappears. You are not lost — you know exactly where you are — but the cold has reached the place inside your chest where decisions live, and you cannot quite remember why you were walking. You should sit down. You should rest. The snow is soft. The cold is not painful anymore; it is becoming warm, which you know — in the part of your mind that is still thinking — means you are dying. And then she is there: standing in the snow without footprints, her skin white as the snow-field, her hair black as the gap between stars, and her eyes the pale blue of deep ice. She is the most beautiful thing you have ever seen. She reaches for you with both hands. Her touch is the last cold you will ever feel.

### Combat

A yuki-onna does not ambush — she *appears*. She manifests during blizzards or deep-winter nights when travellers are already cold, exhausted, and vulnerable. Snow-blindness gaze disorients; breath of winter saps strength; and frozen embrace is the killing mechanism — a sustained touch that drains Constitution until the victim freezes to death.

She is a patience-predator. She does not chase. She waits for the cold to do most of the work, then finishes what the blizzard started.

### Special Abilities

**Frozen Embrace (Su):** A yuki-onna's touch attack deals 1d6 cold damage plus 1d4 Constitution damage (DC 19 Fortitude negates the Con damage). A creature reduced to 0 Constitution by this ability freezes solid — perfectly preserved, as if sleeping. The save DC is Charisma-based (includes Ability Focus).

**Snow-Blindness Gaze (Su):** 30-foot range. Creatures that meet a yuki-onna's gaze must succeed on a DC 17 Will save or be dazzled for 1d4 rounds (seeing only white, as if snow-blind). Creatures that fail by 5 or more are blinded for 1 round. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Breath of Winter (Su):** 3/day, a yuki-onna exhales a 30-foot cone of freezing air. 4d6 cold damage (DC 17 Reflex half) and creatures that fail the save are fatigued for 1d4 rounds (or exhausted if already fatigued). The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Snow Stride (Ex):** A yuki-onna moves across snow and ice at full speed, leaving no tracks. She is unaffected by snow-based difficult terrain.

**Vanish in Snow (Su):** In falling snow or blizzard conditions, a yuki-onna can become invisible as a move action. This functions as *greater invisibility* but only while snow is actively falling.

**Frozen Grief Resonance (Su):** A yuki-onna registers on Kin-sense as frozen undeath — the elemental resonance of the original person preserved but unreachable, encased in cold. Detectable at 30 feet. Hasura describe it as "hearing someone singing beneath ice" — a muffled, beautiful, profoundly sad signal that pulls at the listener's empathy. DC 18 Will save to resist the impulse to approach.

### Society

Yuki-onna are the undead of Hasura winter — women (always women, in Hasura tradition) who died of cold in the northern forests and rose again as spirits of frozen grief. They are among the most culturally significant undead in Oreka because Hasura culture's relationship with them is complex, sorrowful, and not entirely adversarial.

Hasura communities in the northern forests maintain yuki-onna protocols that are distinct from all other anti-undead traditions in Oreka. A yuki-onna is not a monster to be destroyed — she is a person who died badly and needs help moving on. Hasura priests who encounter a yuki-onna attempt first to *speak* to her, learning her name and the circumstances of her death. Many yuki-onna retain fragments of their living personality and will respond to conversation, especially if addressed by their living name.

A yuki-onna who is reminded of who she was — who hears her name spoken with genuine recognition — may choose to dissipate voluntarily, releasing the frozen grief that animates her. Hasura priests call this *yukimokure* — "snow-release" — and consider it the proper resolution. Destroying a yuki-onna through combat or turning is regarded as a failure, not a victory.

Some yuki-onna spare travellers. Hasura oral tradition records yuki-onna who released victims after being shown kindness — a shared cloak, a warm drink offered to the snow, or simply spoken-to as a person rather than a threat. Whether this is the yuki-onna's living personality surfacing or an unpredictable quirk of the undead condition is debated.

### Lore

**DC 15 (Knowledge [religion]):** Yuki-onna are Medium undead of northern winter-forests. Cold immunity, fire vulnerability. Frozen embrace drains Constitution. They appear during blizzards and vanish in snow. Always women who died of cold.

**DC 20:** Snow-blindness gaze disorients; breath of winter fatigues. Hasura traditions prioritise speaking to yuki-onna rather than fighting — learning her name and offering *yukimokure* (snow-release). Some yuki-onna spare travellers who show kindness.

**DC 25:** Frozen grief resonance pulls at empathy (DC 18 Will). Destroying a yuki-onna through combat is considered a failure in Hasura culture. The oldest yuki-onna (10+ HD) can freeze a person solid with a single embrace.

# ZASHIKI-WARASHI

You will not see it. That is the first thing to understand. You will hear it — small feet on wooden floors, a child's giggle behind a screen, the sound of something being moved in a room where no one is standing. You will feel it — the house is warm, the food tastes better, the neighbours are kind. The garden grows. The baby sleeps through the night. The family prospers. And you will never, not once, see the zashiki-warashi — but it is there, has always been there, and the moment it leaves, you will understand what prosperity felt like, because it will be gone.

### Combat

A zashiki-warashi does not fight. It has no attacks, no offensive abilities, and no combat instincts. If threatened, it uses permanent invisibility and its extraordinary stealth to avoid detection, and if the threat is sustained (a hostile presence in the house, violence, or cruelty), it simply *leaves* — which is, in Tanuki understanding, the worst outcome imaginable.

### Special Abilities

**Permanent Invisibility (Su):** A zashiki-warashi is permanently invisible, as *greater invisibility*. This cannot be suppressed voluntarily — the zashiki-warashi is unseen by nature. *See invisibility* and *true seeing* can detect it: a small, childlike figure in simple clothing, always barefoot, always looking slightly away from the observer. Even detected, it will flee rather than interact directly.

**Childlike Presence (Su):** Creatures within the zashiki-warashi's house hear occasional sounds of a child — laughter, footsteps, small objects being moved. These sounds are not illusions; they are the zashiki-warashi's natural activity. Creatures that hear these sounds and respond with warmth (speaking kindly, leaving small offerings of food or toys) strengthen the house blessing.

**House Blessing (Su):** A zashiki-warashi that inhabits a structure for more than one month grants the following benefits to all friendly residents: +1 morale bonus on saving throws, +2 bonus on Profession checks conducted within the house, and food prepared in the household is nourishing enough to provide the benefits of a *heroes' feast* once per week (minus the combat benefits — only the nourishment and immunity to poison/fear). The blessing intensifies with longer residence: after one year, saving throw bonus increases to +2.

**House Withdrawal (Su):** When a zashiki-warashi leaves, the house's fortune reverses within one week: crops fail, trade sours, minor accidents accumulate, and the morale of the household deteriorates. This is not a curse — it is the absence of a blessing. The reversal fades over one month as the household returns to its natural baseline.

**Hearthstone Warmth Resonance (Su):** A zashiki-warashi registers on Kin-sense as domestic earth — the warmth of a good foundation, the comfort of stone walls in winter. Detectable at 15 feet. Experienced as a feeling of *home* — Kin-sense users in a zashiki-warashi's house feel safe, welcome, and cared for. The resonance cannot be distinguished from the general warmth of a well-loved house, which is why most residents never realise a fey is responsible.

### Society

Zashiki-warashi are the household fey of Tanuki culture — invisible child-spirits that inhabit well-maintained homes and bring prosperity to the families who live there. They are among the most benign creatures in Oreka, with no offensive capabilities and no hostile intent; their entire existence is the care and nurturing of a single household.

Tanuki communities understand zashiki-warashi as a natural part of domestic life. A Tanuki lodge that has been well-maintained for three or more generations may attract a zashiki-warashi; the signs are gradual (better harvests, fewer illnesses, a general sense of good fortune) and the Tanuki response is not to investigate but to *continue doing what they're doing* — maintaining the house, treating each other well, and leaving small offerings of rice-cakes and wooden toys in the house's quietest room.

The greatest Tanuki domestic fear is *driving a zashiki-warashi away*. Household cruelty (violence between family members, neglect of the house, mistreatment of guests) causes the zashiki-warashi to leave, and the resulting reversal of fortune is Tanuki culture's most powerful cautionary tale. "You'll chase the warashi away" is a Tanuki reprimand for selfishness.

Tsukumogami (animated household objects) sometimes share houses with zashiki-warashi, and the two fey-types are complementary — the tsukumogami are the house's *memory*; the zashiki-warashi is the house's *fortune*.

### Lore

**DC 11 (Knowledge [nature]):** Zashiki-warashi are Small, invisible fey that inhabit well-maintained houses in Tanuki territory. They bring prosperity to the household and are never seen. They have no attacks.

**DC 16:** House blessing provides +1 saves, +2 Profession, and weekly nourishing meals. The blessing intensifies with longer residence. If the zashiki-warashi leaves, fortune reverses for one month. Household cruelty drives them away.

**DC 21:** Zashiki-warashi and tsukumogami are complementary household fey. Hearthstone warmth resonance is indistinguishable from a well-loved house. Tanuki leave offerings of rice-cakes and wooden toys in the house's quietest room.

# ZMEY GORYNYCH

Three heads. That is what the Rarozhki scouts reported, and three heads is what you see: three serpentine necks rising from a body built like a fortress of red-black scales, each head armoured, horned, and breathing independently. The left head watches you. The centre head watches the sky. The right head watches the tunnel behind it. Nothing approaches a zmey gorynych from a single direction, because the zmey gorynych is watching every direction simultaneously. Each mouth trails smoke. Each eye holds the intelligence of a true dragon — not three separate minds but one mind expressed through three independent awareness-points, a tactical consciousness that processes three threat-vectors at once and responds to all of them.

### Combat

A zmey gorynych is a three-front combatant. Triple awareness means it cannot be flanked, surprised, or caught watching the wrong direction. Three independent breath weapons mean it can cover three separate arcs in the same round. Three bite attacks target three different opponents, and the dragon's tactical intelligence ensures it prioritises the most dangerous target with the centre head while the flanking heads suppress secondary threats.

Opening tactics: frightful presence (60-ft. radius, DC 21 Will or panicked/shaken), then breath weapons to scatter the party, then close to melee against isolated targets. A zmey gorynych that is outmatched retreats to its volcanic lair — the heat-zone alone kills pursuit.

### Special Abilities

**Breath Weapon (Su):** Each head breathes independently — a 40-foot cone of fire, 6d6 fire damage (DC 22 Reflex half, Constitution-based). Each head can use its breath weapon once every 1d4 rounds. In a full attack, all three heads may breathe in the same round if available, each targeting a separate 40-foot cone. The combined coverage is devastating against spread-out parties.

**Frightful Presence (Ex):** 60-foot radius. Creatures of fewer than 15 HD must succeed on a DC 21 Will save or become panicked (fewer than 6 HD) or shaken (6+ HD) for 4d6 rounds. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Head Regeneration (Ex):** A severed zmey gorynych head (requires a called shot at –4, dealing 25+ damage in a single blow against the head's AC 30) regrows in 1d4+1 rounds. While a head is missing, the zmey gorynych loses one bite attack and one breath weapon arc. Only fire damage applied to the stump prevents regrowth — ironic, given the creature's fire immunity; alchemical fire, holy fire, or fire from a weapon specifically enchanted to bypass fire immunity is required.

**Triple Awareness (Ex):** A zmey gorynych cannot be flanked and gains a +4 bonus on Search, Spot, and Listen checks (already included). It processes three independent visual fields simultaneously and can track three separate moving targets without penalty.

**Rend (Ex):** If two or more bite attacks hit the same target in a round, the zmey gorynych rends for an additional 2d8+12 damage.

**Volcanic Intelligence Resonance (Su):** A zmey gorynych registers on Kin-sense as three-fold fire — the Lady of Fire's domain expressed through three simultaneous awareness-points. Detectable at 60 feet. The resonance is experienced as three overlapping heat-signatures, each slightly different in character (one aggressive, one watchful, one calculating), which is disorienting — Kin-sense check DC 20 to parse the reading accurately. Rarozhki who have encountered zmey gorynych describe the resonance as "the Lady arguing with herself."

### Society

Zmey gorynych are the Scorchspires' apex predator-dragons — three-headed, fire-immune, volcanically intelligent hunters that occupy the ecological tier between Oreka's true chromatic/metallic dragons and the Emberfang Wyrm sovereign-tier creatures. They are not as ancient or as powerful as Emberfang Wyrms, but they are far more numerous, far more aggressive, and far more likely to be encountered.

Rarozhki dwarves distinguish sharply between ember drakes (sacred, welcomed), Emberfang Wyrms (sacred beyond measure, never disturbed), and zmey gorynych (dangerous, respected, avoided). A zmey gorynych is not sacred — it is a predator that happens to share the Lady of Fire's domain. Rarozhki mining-operations in the Scorchspires' inner range map zmey gorynych territories carefully and route around them. Direct confrontation is avoided unless the zmey gorynych threatens a forge-complex, in which case the Rarozhki response is a full clan-muster — a zmey gorynych requires overwhelming force to drive off.

Zmey gorynych are intelligent enough for negotiation (Int 16, speak Draconic and Ignan) and occasionally demand tribute from Rarozhki settlements in exchange for territorial non-aggression — livestock, refined metals, and most valued of all, embersteel. The negotiations are conducted through intermediaries; no Rarozhki sends a delegate into a zmey gorynych's lair.

Mated pairs of zmey gorynych are among the most dangerous encounters in the Scorchspires — six heads, six breath weapons, coordinated by two tactical minds sharing a lair.

### Lore

**DC 16 (Knowledge [arcana]):** Zmey gorynych are three-headed fire-dragons of the Scorchspires. Each head breathes independently (40-ft. cone, 6d6 fire). DR 10/magic, SR 24, fire immune. Cannot be flanked.

**DC 21:** Severed heads regenerate in 1d4+1 rounds unless the stump is cauterised with fire that bypasses fire immunity. Triple hoard (gold, weapons, scrolls). Intelligent enough for negotiation in Draconic and Ignan.

**DC 26:** Three-fold resonance ("the Lady arguing with herself") is disorienting on Kin-sense. Rarozhki pay tribute to avoid territorial conflict. Mated pairs coordinate six heads and are among the Scorchspires' most dangerous encounters. Not sacred — respected as predators, not venerated.

# ZOMBIE

It walks. That is the horror of it — not the rot, not the smell, not the exposed bone or the grey-green skin or the blank, staring eyes. The horror is that it *walks*. It was a person. You can see the person it was — the build, the clothing, the face. And now it walks toward you with its arms out and its mouth open and no thought behind the eyes at all. Nothing. Not hunger, not malice, not pain. Nothing. It is meat that remembers how to move and has forgotten everything else.

### Combat

A zombie fights by advancing and slamming. It has no tactics, no self-preservation, and no ability to adapt. Single actions only — a zombie can move or attack each round, not both (unless it uses a partial charge). This limitation makes individual zombies trivial threats and zombie mobs slow, predictable, and devastating through sheer numbers.

DR 5/slashing means piercing and bludgeoning weapons are partially ineffective — a fact that catches unprepared adventurers off-guard. The proper response to zombies is edged weapons and fire.

### Special Abilities

**Single Actions Only (Ex):** A zombie can take only a single move action or standard action each round (it still gets a partial charge).

**Rotting Absence Resonance (Su):** A zombie registers on Kin-sense as the lowest form of undead presence — dead tissue animated without will, hunger, or identity. Detectable at 15 feet. The reading is unmistakable (no living creature produces so flat and empty a signal) but carries no information beyond "undead." A mob of twenty zombies reads as a single smear of absence rather than twenty distinct sources.

### Society

Zombies have no society. They are the lowest-order undead — mindless, instinct-free, and useful only as disposable labour for necromancers, Dómnathar commanders, and other creatures with the ability to control undead.

In Oreka, zombies appear most commonly in three contexts. First, as natural-rising undead at mass-death sites where necromantic contamination saturates the ground — the Reckoning-era battlefields of the Kinsweave produce occasional zombie-emergence when heavy rain exposes buried remains. Second, as Dómnathar terror-troops, animated from captured corpses and deployed ahead of actual Dómnathar forces to absorb defences, exhaust resources, and demoralise settlements. Third, as the incidental product of other necromantic events — a lich's laboratory, a cursed barrow, or a botched resurrection spell.

Every Kin culture treats zombie-prevention as fundamental. Eruskan cremation, Vestri stone-sealing, Mytroan sky-burial, Hasura forest-interment (specific to tree-hollow placement that encourages decomposition), Kovaka reef-burial, and Orean mountain-interment all prevent or significantly reduce zombie formation through different mechanisms. The Dómnathar's use of zombies is considered particularly offensive because it reverses these cultural protections — taking bodies that were properly interred and raising them anyway, which is experienced by the victim's culture as a violation of the dead's dignity and the living's care.

### Lore

**DC 10 (Knowledge [religion]):** Zombies are mindless undead. They are slow (single actions only), tough (DR 5/slashing), and dangerous only in large numbers. They obey the last command given to them.

**DC 15:** Zombies arise naturally at mass-death sites or are created by necromancers. Every Kin culture has burial traditions to prevent zombie formation. Dómnathar use zombie-troops as expendable terror-weapons. Slashing weapons and fire are the proper countermeasures.
