# WATER ELEMENTAL

The river rises. Not floods — the water level does not change — but a column of river-water pulls itself upright, shaping limbs and a torso and something that might be a head from the current itself. It stands on the river's surface like a person stands on a floor, and within its translucent body you can see river-debris turning slowly: pebbles, weeds, a small fish swimming in confused circles. When it moves, the sound is rain on stone.

Water elementals are living currents — portions of Oreka's rivers, seas, and lakes given purpose and form. They range from knee-high ripples to towering columns of pressurised water that can crush stone. In Oreka, they are the Lady of the Sea's most direct expression — water given will.

### Combat

Water elementals fight by engulfing and overwhelming. In water, they are devastating — water mastery grants +1 on attack and damage against any creature touching water. On land, they are significantly slower (20 ft.) but still formidable. Their vortex ability (as whirlwind but in water) traps and drowns opponents.

### Special Abilities

**Water Mastery (Ex):** A water elemental gains +1 on attack and damage rolls if both it and its opponent are touching water. If the opponent or the elemental is touching the ground, the elemental takes –4 on attack and damage rolls.

**Drench (Ex):** The elemental's touch extinguishes nonmagical fires of its size category or smaller. Against magical fire, the elemental makes a dispel check (1d20 + HD) against each spell.

**Vortex (Su):** As whirlwind but functions only in water. The elemental can transform into a vortex once every 10 minutes and remain in that form for up to 1 round per 2 HD.

**Lady of the Sea Resonance (Su):** Water elementals register on Kin-sense as the Lady of the Sea's domain made animate — cool, rhythmic, like a pulse felt through water. The intensity scales with size: Small elementals produce a gentle, soothing current-sense at 15 feet; Elder elementals produce a crushing oceanic pressure detectable at 120 feet. Pekakarlik water-priests describe Elder water elementals as "the Lady walking."

### Society

Water elementals are expressions of the Lady of the Sea's domain — living currents given purpose. They are most commonly encountered in the Great River system, where Pekakarlik water-priests maintain summoning-traditions dating back centuries, and in the Kailian and Apelian Seas, where water elementals arise spontaneously during major storms.

Eruskan river-cities occasionally experience spontaneous water elemental manifestation during the Great River's flood-season — the sheer volume and energy of floodwater can produce unsummoned elementals that are confused, destructive, and difficult to dismiss.

Na'wasua sea-elves have the deepest natural relationship with water elementals — marine water elementals in Na'wasua territory respond to Na'wasua qareen-resonance with a recognition that suggests the Lady's domain remembers its own.

### Lore

**DC 13 (Knowledge [the planes]):** Water elementals are living water given form. They gain combat advantages in water and are slower on land. Their vortex ability traps and drowns opponents. They are the Lady of the Sea's most direct expression.

**DC 18:** Water mastery gives +1 attack/damage in water. Drench extinguishes fires. Pekakarlik water-priests maintain summoning-traditions. Spontaneous manifestation occurs during Great River floods.

### Combat

Water mephits fight with claw attacks and breath weapons — a 15-foot cone of caustic water (1d8 acid, DC 13 Reflex half). They are individually weak but annoying in groups. Their primary tactic is to use *stinking cloud* to nauseate a party, then dive-bomb with claws while the targets are retching.

### Special Abilities

**Breath Weapon (Su):** 15-foot cone of caustic water, 1d8 acid damage (DC 13 Reflex half, Constitution-based). Usable once every 1d4 rounds.

**Spell-Like Abilities:** Caster level 3rd.
- 1/day — *stinking cloud* (DC 15), *acid arrow*
- 1/hour — *summon mephit* (1 water mephit, 25% success)

**Mist-Mischief Resonance (Su):** Water mephits register on Kin-sense as trivial water — river-mist with attitude, the Lady's domain at its pettiest. Detectable at 15 feet. Eruskan rivermen describe it as "the fog being rude."

### Society

Water mephits are the Great River's pest-elementals — small, malicious, fundamentally harmless outsiders that inhabit the mist-zones along the river's banks. They harass fishermen, steal food from riverside camps, and spit caustic water at anyone who annoys them, which is everyone.

Eruskan river-folk treat them as weather — unavoidable, irritating, and manageable with the right precautions (covered food-stores, closed windows during mist-season). Vodyanoy consider water mephits beneath their notice and occasionally use them as reluctant messengers.

### Lore

**DC 13 (Knowledge [the planes]):** Water mephits are Small water-outsiders found along the Great River. Acid breath weapon, *stinking cloud*. Individually weak, annoying in groups. Fast healing near water.

**DC 18:** Vodyanoy sometimes use them as messengers. Eruskan treat them as seasonal weather-annoyance. Mobs of 5–12 can emerge during flood-mist season.

The naga is coiled on the submerged ledge like a crown of emerald-green scales, its humanoid face composed, watchful, and radiating an intelligence that is not hostile but is absolutely, unmistakably *evaluating* you. It is deciding whether you are a threat, a resource, or an interesting conversation.

### Combat

A water naga is a defensive combatant — it protects its pool, not aggressively but firmly. Against intruders, it uses spells first (*mirror image*, *protection from energy*) to establish defence, then *suggestion* to redirect intruders away from the pool. Poison (bite, DC 17 Fort, 1d8 Con initial and secondary) is used against persistent aggressors.

### Special Abilities

**Poison (Ex):** Bite; Fortitude DC 17; initial and secondary 1d8 Con. The save DC is Constitution-based.

**Spells:** A water naga casts spells as a 7th-level sorcerer:
- 0 — *daze, detect magic, light, mage hand, open/close, ray of frost, read magic*
- 1st (7/day) — *magic missile, shield, true strike, mage armour*
- 2nd (7/day) — *mirror image, invisibility, acid arrow*
- 3rd (5/day) — *protection from energy, suggestion* (DC 17)

**Guardian Current Resonance (Su):** A water naga registers on Kin-sense as protective water — the Lady of the Sea's domain in watchful, guarding mode. Detectable at 30 feet. The contrast with a spirit naga's corrupted-current is stark: where a spirit naga's water feels *claimed*, a water naga's feels *protected*. Pekakarlik river-navigators can distinguish between the two, which is critical for determining whether a deep pool harbours a guardian (water naga) or a predator (spirit naga).

### Society

Water nagas are the Great River's benign pool-guardians — intelligent aberrations that claim specific deep pools and defend them against corruption, pollution, and predatory creatures. They are the counterpart to the spirit naga: where spirit nagas corrupt water, water nagas protect it.

Pekakarlik river-communities maintain cautiously respectful relationships with water nagas. A pool guarded by a water naga is safe water — clean, fish-rich, and free of spirit nagas, tuchulcha, and other water-predators. Pekakarlik fishermen who wish to access water naga pools request permission through ritual (speaking to the water, explaining intent), similar to vodyanoy protocols but less formal.

Water nagas and river serpents coexist peacefully — both serve the Lady of the Sea's protective aspect.

### Lore

**DC 17 (Knowledge [arcana]):** Water nagas are Large, intelligent pool-guardians of the Great River. They cast spells as 7th-level sorcerers and deliver Constitution-damaging poison. They are territorial but not hostile to respectful visitors.

**DC 22:** Water naga pools are clean and fish-rich — the opposite of spirit naga corruption. Pekakarlik distinguish water naga and spirit naga pools through Kin-sense resonance. Water nagas coexist peacefully with river serpents.

# WENDIGO

The hunger comes before the creature does. You feel it first in your stomach — a hollow, gnawing emptiness that has nothing to do with whether you've eaten — and then in your mind, where it becomes something worse: the certainty that nothing will ever fill you, that the emptiness is permanent, and that the only thing that might — *might* — ease it is the warmth of another living body, broken open. The trees above creak. Something enormous moves through the canopy with a speed that should be impossible for its size, and when you see it — the long limbs, the antlered skull, the mouth that is a vertical gash of frozen teeth — you understand that the hunger you feel is not yours. It is the wendigo's. And it is sharing.

### Combat

A wendigo hunts from the air — flying silently above the canopy, descending to strike with Flyby Attack, and withdrawing before retaliation. The howl is the opening weapon: a 60-foot cone of supernatural terror that panics targets, separating them from their group. Once a target is isolated, the wendigo descends to feed.

Wendigo hunger is the creature's defining attack: each claw strike delivers cold damage plus a hunger-curse that erodes the victim's will. A creature that fails the save begins to feel the wendigo's insatiable emptiness, and if the hunger accumulates to a critical threshold, the victim is compelled to flee into the wilderness to "feed" — a compulsion that leads to death by exposure.

### Special Abilities

**Wendigo Hunger (Su):** A creature struck by a wendigo's claw must succeed on a DC 20 Will save or gain one level of wendigo hunger. At three levels, the creature is compelled to flee into the wilderness (as *confusion* with only the "flee" result) for 1d4 hours. A creature that flees takes 1d6 cold damage per round from the wendigo's supernatural chill. *Remove curse* (DC 22) removes all hunger levels. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Howl (Su):** Once per minute, a wendigo can release a howl in a 60-foot cone. Creatures in the cone must succeed on a DC 20 Will save or be panicked for 1d4 rounds. Creatures that fail by 5 or more are also affected by *confusion* for 1 round. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Windwalk Possession (Su):** Once per day, a wendigo can attempt to possess a creature that has reached three levels of wendigo hunger. The target must succeed on a DC 20 Will save or be possessed (as *magic jar* with no receptacle — the wendigo enters the body directly). A possessed creature transforms over 1d4 days into a new wendigo, destroying the original soul. *Dispel evil* (DC 20) or *break enchantment* can interrupt the transformation if cast within the first 24 hours. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Regeneration 5 (Ex):** Fire damage is not regenerated.

**Frozen Wind Resonance (Su):** A wendigo registers on Kin-sense as the Youngest Brother's domain corrupted — wind stripped of warmth, air that carries hunger instead of life. Detectable at 120 feet. The resonance is immediately recognisable by wind-attuned Kin as *wrong* — Pasua scouts and Mytroan wind-callers describe it as "the Brother screaming." The resonance chills Kin-sense itself — prolonged exposure (more than 1 minute) imposes –2 on all Kin-sense checks for 1 hour.

### Society

Wendigo are the apex predators of the Gatefall and Kinsweave forests — solitary, ancient, and terrifying outsiders that embody hunger itself. They are not Dómnathar-created (they predate the Dómnathar's arrival) but they thrive in Dómnathar-influenced zones where the elemental fabric is weakened and the natural order is stressed.

Kinsweave border-kingdom communities maintain elaborate anti-wendigo protocols. Never travel alone in deep winter. Never eat less than your share (hunger weakens the will, and a weakened will is vulnerable to wendigo hunger). If you hear the howl, do not run — running isolates you. Stand together, make fire, and wait for dawn.

Pasua scouts who range through Gatefall Reach consider wendigo the most dangerous creature in their operating territory. Pasua anti-wendigo training emphasises fire: fire-arrows, alchemist's fire, and the ability to create a defensive fire-ring rapidly. A wendigo will not approach a sustained fire.

### Lore

**DC 18 (Knowledge [the planes]):** Wendigo are Large, flying outsiders of deep cold-forests. They are cold-immune and fire-vulnerable. Regeneration 5 (fire). Their howl panics. Their claw attacks deliver a hunger-curse that compels victims to flee into the wilderness.

**DC 23:** Wendigo hunger accumulates through failed saves — at three levels, the victim is compelled to flee. *Remove curse* (DC 22) clears all levels. Windwalk possession transforms the victim into a new wendigo over 1d4 days. DR 10/cold iron and fire.

**DC 28:** Wendigo resonance is the Youngest Brother's domain corrupted into hungry cold. They predate the Dómnathar but thrive in Dómnathar-weakened zones. Pasua scouts carry fire as primary countermeasure. Kinsweave anti-wendigo protocols: never travel alone, never eat less than your share.
