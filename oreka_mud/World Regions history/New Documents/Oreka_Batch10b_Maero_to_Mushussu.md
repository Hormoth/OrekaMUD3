# MAERO (BRUTE)

The Kovaka call them *te tangata mohoao* — the wild people — but there is nothing of a person in what steps out of the tree-line. It is eight feet of matted hair and raw-boned muscle, its arms too long for its body, its hands thick-fingered and blunt as mallets. The face is wrong in the particular way that all Brute faces are wrong: close enough to Kin to trigger recognition, far enough from Kin to trigger revulsion. It smells of rotting bark and swamp-water and something chemical beneath both — the faint, sour reek that every Dómnathar product carries if you know what to look for. The maero watches you from the forest edge with dull, unblinking eyes, and then it reaches up and tears a branch the thickness of your thigh from the nearest tree as casually as you would pluck a flower.

### Combat

A maero is the forest-adapted Brute — built by the Dómnathar for the same role as the Goreham (attrition and shock) but optimised for dense terrain. Where the Goreham is slow and heavily armoured, the maero is faster, more agile, and equipped with a climb speed that allows it to attack from unexpected angles in forest environments. It is still fundamentally a blunt instrument — maero charge, hit, and rend — but the forest setting makes them more dangerous than their CR suggests because they ambush from above, swinging down from canopy branches onto targets in the undergrowth.

A maero that hits with both slam attacks in the same round rends, tearing with its oversized hands. Against fortified positions, maero tear apart wooden structures with frightening speed — palisades, tree-platforms, and even Kovaka longhouses are vulnerable. A maero retreats into the deep forest if reduced below 20 hit points, climbing into the canopy where pursuit is difficult.

### Special Abilities

**Rend (Ex):** If a maero hits with both slam attacks in the same round, it latches on and tears, dealing an additional 2d6+10 damage.

**Forest Adaptation (Ex):** A maero has a climb speed of 20 ft. and receives a +8 racial bonus on Climb checks (can always take 10). It can brachiate through forest canopy at its climb speed, swinging from branch to branch without needing to descend. In forested terrain, a maero receives a +8 racial bonus on Hide checks.

**Hollow Resonance (Su):** A maero registers on Kin-sense as a hollow, buzzing imitation of earth-attunement — the signature of a Dómnathar-manufactured creature carrying artificial elemental resonance. The effect is recognisably fake: Kovaka elves describe it as "moss growing on a stone that was never in the forest." The hollow resonance is detectable at 60 feet (DC 12 Kin-sense check to identify as Brute rather than natural creature; DC 10 for Kovaka, who know the difference from generations of coexistence). Unlike Dómnathar void-silence, hollow resonance does not suppress Kin-sense — it merely reads as wrong.

**Dómnathar-Forged (Su):** The maero is a Dómnathar war-product, manufactured in the flesh-forges during or before the Deceivers' War and released into the wild during the army's passage through the Gatefall Reach. Maero are not produced any more — the flesh-forges that created them are either destroyed or inaccessible. Existing maero are War-era survivors or, rarely, their offspring. Unlike Hrokk, maero reproduce infrequently and poorly — litters are small, infant mortality is high, and the population is slowly declining.

### Society

Maero are the Brutes of the Kovaka forests — feral, Dómnathar-manufactured war-creatures that escaped or were abandoned during the Deceivers' War and have persisted in the dense mountain valleys of southwestern Oreka for three centuries. They are the least numerous of the Brute types (perhaps forty to sixty individuals remain), but they are disproportionately dangerous in their preferred terrain because the forest gives them every advantage.

Kovaka elves have the longest and most painful relationship with maero. The Kovaka forests were not a major battleground during the War itself — the maero were scattered into the valleys during the Lost Third's passage south, abandoned by Dómnathar handlers who needed them as a rearguard and did not return. Three hundred years later, the Kovaka regard maero as the War's most persistent local scar. Kovaka settlements in maero-adjacent valleys maintain *tangata-kaitiaki* (Brute-watchers) — trained scouts who monitor maero territories, track population changes, and organise community defence when a maero encroaches on settled land.

The Kovaka approach to maero management is deliberate restraint. They do not attempt to exterminate the remaining population (the Kovaka cultural philosophy of sustainable coexistence extends even to Brutes), but they do not allow maero to establish territories within a day's walk of settlements. When a maero crosses this boundary, the Kovaka response is swift and professional: a team of six to eight hunters using coordinated tactics developed specifically for forest-Brute engagements. The mortality rate for these hunts is not zero.

Other Kin cultures have minimal contact with maero. Pasua ground-elves occasionally encounter maero at the forest-steppe border and treat them as they would any dangerous predator — avoid, report, and let the Kovaka handle it. Eruskan traders who use the coastal routes through Kovaka territory are warned about maero at every waystation.

### Lore

**DC 16 (Knowledge [nature]):** Maero are Large, hairy, forest-dwelling Brutes — Dómnathar-manufactured war-creatures, not true giants. They climb trees, ambush from above, and rend with both hands. They are feral and territorial.

**DC 21:** Maero have hollow resonance on Kin-sense — recognisably fake earth-attunement. They reproduce poorly and the population is declining. Kovaka elves maintain Brute-watchers who track maero territories. Slashing and fire are effective against them.

**DC 26:** Maero were scattered into the Kovaka valleys during the Lost Third's passage south. Approximately forty to sixty remain. Kovaka *tangata-kaitiaki* (Brute-watchers) use coordinated forest-engagement tactics to manage the population — these are among the most skilled Brute-hunters in Oreka.

It is two feet tall, vaguely humanoid, and made of cooling magma — each movement cracking its skin to reveal the orange glow beneath. It grins at you with a mouth full of obsidian teeth and speaks in a voice like a campfire settling. Behind it, three more are climbing out.

### Combat

Magma mephits attack in groups whenever possible, using their flight to stay out of melee reach while pelting targets with magma blobs. If forced into melee, they rely on their fire-damage claws and the knowledge that most opponents will take damage simply from sustained contact. A magma mephit reduced to half hit points retreats to the nearest volcanic vent, where its fast healing activates.

### Special Abilities

**Breath Weapon (Su):** 10-foot cone of molten rock, once every 1d4 rounds, 1d4 fire damage, Reflex DC 12 half. The magma sticks — a creature that fails the save takes an additional 1d4 fire damage on the following round. The save DC is Constitution-based.

**Magma Blob (Sp):** Once per day, a magma mephit can hurl a blob of magma as a ranged touch attack (30 ft. range), dealing 1d6 fire damage plus 1d6 fire damage on the following round as the magma cools.

**Summon Mephit (Sp):** Once per day, a magma mephit can attempt to summon another magma mephit (25% chance of success). This ability functions as a 2nd-level spell.

**Fire-Earth Resonance (Su):** Magma mephits register on Kin-sense as a small, hot, irritable fire-earth signature — detectable at 30 feet. Rarozhki dwarves describe them as "the Scorchspires having a tantrum." The resonance is familiar to any Kin who has spent time near volcanic terrain and carries no particular threat-signature — it reads as "minor elemental nuisance."

### Society

Magma mephits are the Scorchspires' most common elemental inhabitants — small, hot-tempered, and present in irritating numbers near every active vent and lava tube. They are semi-intelligent (Int 7) and capable of crude conversation in Ignan and broken Common, though their conversational range is limited to complaints, demands, insults, and negotiations for food.

Rarozhki dwarves regard magma mephits as a constant, low-level hazard of volcanic life — the Scorchspire equivalent of rats. Rarozhki smithing-halls near active vents maintain mephit-wards (cold-iron grates over vent-openings) and keep supplies of water and ice on hand for mephit-dispersal. Individual mephits that prove unusually cooperative are sometimes tolerated as forge-assistants — their ability to maintain high temperatures in confined spaces has genuine industrial value, and Rarozhki smiths have been known to pay mephits in raw ore and obsidian chips for their services.

### Lore

**DC 13 (Knowledge [the planes]):** Magma mephits are Small fire-earth outsiders found near volcanic vents. They breathe cones of molten rock, hurl magma blobs, and are immune to fire but vulnerable to cold.

**DC 18:** Magma mephits heal rapidly near volcanic terrain (fast healing 2). They can summon additional mephits once per day. Rarozhki dwarves consider them pests but occasionally employ cooperative individuals as forge-assistants.

**DC 23:** Magma mephits are the most numerous elemental outsiders in the Scorchspires, serving as inadvertent indicators of volcanic activity — a sudden increase in mephit population signals rising vent temperatures. Their fire-earth resonance is distinct from but related to cherufe and magmin signatures.

# MAGMIN

The lava-tube glows from within — not the dull red of cooling rock, but the bright, shifting orange of something moving. A shape detaches itself from the wall: small, humanoid, and burning. Not on fire — *made* of fire, given form by a thin shell of cooling rock that cracks and reforms with every step. Where it walks, the stone beneath its feet softens and glows. It looks at you with eyes that are holes into something incandescent, and you understand instantly that this creature does not know it is dangerous. It simply *is* dangerous, the way a campfire is dangerous — not through malice, but through nature.

### Combat

Magmin are not tacticians. They engage in melee immediately, slamming into opponents with incandescent fists. Their primary threat is not their damage output but their combustion and melt weapons abilities — anything flammable that touches a magmin ignites, and metal weapons that strike it risk melting. Fighting a magmin in close quarters is a war of attrition that destroys equipment faster than it destroys the magmin.

### Special Abilities

**Combustion (Ex):** Any creature hit by a magmin's slam must succeed on a DC 12 Reflex save or catch fire. Burning creatures take 1d6 fire damage per round until the fire is extinguished (full-round action, DC 15 Reflex). The save DC is Constitution-based.

**Fiery Aura (Ex):** A magmin radiates intense heat. Any creature within 5 feet takes 1 point of fire damage per round (no save). Flammable materials within 5 feet ignite automatically.

**Melt Weapons (Ex):** Any metal weapon that strikes a magmin must succeed on a DC 12 Fortitude save or melt (becoming useless). Magic weapons apply their enhancement bonus to this save. The save DC is Constitution-based.

**Fire Resonance (Su):** A magmin registers on Kin-sense as a small, bright point of pure fire-resonance — warm and uncomplicated, without malice or deception. Rarozhki dwarves describe it as "a candle-flame that walks." The resonance is detectable at 30 feet and is unmistakable — there is nothing else in Oreka that reads as pure, unalloyed elemental fire at this scale. The warmth it radiates on Kin-sense is actually pleasant at distances beyond 20 feet, which is part of why magmin are so dangerous — they feel welcoming until they are close enough to ignite you.

### Society

Magmin are living fragments of elemental fire given humanoid form — not summoned, not created, but spontaneously generated where volcanic activity intersects with elemental energy. They are simple creatures (Int 8) that exist to burn, explore, and return to magma when cold or tired. They have no culture, no social structure, and no agenda beyond curiosity and the desire to remain warm.

Rarozhki dwarves have learned to coexist with magmin the way one coexists with any natural hazard. Forge-halls near active fissures maintain firebreaks, stone-only construction (no timber within a hundred yards of a known magmin emergence point), and water-reserves for emergency cooling. Individual Rarozhki smiths occasionally develop working relationships with specific magmin — the creatures' ability to maintain extreme temperatures in forge environments is genuinely useful, and a magmin that has been bribed with obsidian and kept warm is a surprisingly cooperative (if hazardous) forge-companion.

Orean humans of the Giant's Teeth foothills consider magmin to be minor fire-spirits and leave offerings of polished stone at known emergence points to discourage the creatures from wandering into settlements.

### Lore

**DC 13 (Knowledge [arcana]):** Magmin are Small fire elementals that ignite anything they touch. Their slam attacks set creatures on fire, and metal weapons that strike them may melt. They are immune to fire and vulnerable to cold.

**DC 18:** Magmin radiate a fiery aura that damages creatures within 5 feet. They are spontaneously generated near volcanic activity. Rarozhki dwarves occasionally employ them as forge-assistants.

**DC 23:** Magmin resonance reads as pure, uncomplicated fire on Kin-sense — deceptively welcoming at a distance. Their generation is tied to the intersection of volcanic activity and elemental energy; increases in magmin population signal rising subterranean temperatures.

# MAMLAMBO

The river crossing has been safe for a decade. The Warg packs ford here every season and the Mytroan riders use it as a watering-point on the steppe-circuit. Then the horses stopped drinking. The Wargs crossed upstream, three miles out of their way, and when asked why, the pack-leader said only: "The river has teeth now." You crouch at the bank and the water looks normal — brown, shallow, running smooth over gravel. You cannot see what is lying on the bottom, because the thing on the bottom has been lying there for weeks, and it has learned what the riverbed looks like, and it is looking back at you.

### Combat

A mamlambo is a patient, ambush-only predator. It lies on the riverbed, perfectly camouflaged, for days or weeks at a time, waiting for creatures to enter the water. When a target wades in — a horse drinking, a rider fording, a herder washing — the mamlambo lunges upward, bites, grabs, and drags the victim underwater. Once submerged, it uses its brain drain ability while holding the victim in its jaws, then swallows the remains.

A mamlambo never fights on land if it can avoid it. If attacked on shore, it retreats to the river immediately. It does not pursue targets onto dry ground. It does not need to — it is patient enough to wait for the next creature that enters the water.

### Special Abilities

**Brain Drain (Su):** A creature bitten by a mamlambo must succeed on a DC 20 Fortitude save or take 1d4 Intelligence damage as the mamlambo's bite dissolves and absorbs neural tissue. A creature reduced to Intelligence 0 by this ability dies. The corpse's face appears smooth and featureless — the mamlambo consumes the brain and face-tissue through its bite. The save DC is Constitution-based.

**Improved Grab (Ex):** Bite attack. A mamlambo that successfully grapples pulls the target underwater on the following round (if in or adjacent to water).

**Swallow Whole (Ex):** A mamlambo can swallow a grabbed Medium or smaller creature with a successful grapple check. A swallowed creature takes 2d6 acid damage plus 1d4 Intelligence damage per round. A swallowed creature can cut its way out with a light slashing weapon by dealing 15 damage to the mamlambo's interior (AC 15). The mamlambo's gullet can hold 1 Medium, 2 Small, or 4 Tiny creatures.

**River Camouflage (Ex):** A mamlambo lying motionless on a riverbed receives a +12 racial bonus on Hide checks in water. Its scales shift colour to match the local river substrate — gravel, mud, sand, or stone. Detecting a submerged, motionless mamlambo requires a DC 26 Spot check.

**Corrupted Water Resonance (Su):** A mamlambo registers on Kin-sense as a corrupted water-signature — stagnant, thick, and predatory. The resonance is detectable at 60 feet, but in flowing river water the ambient water-resonance partially masks the signal (DC 18 to detect in moving water; DC 12 in still pools). Wargs — through the Harmony rather than Kin-sense — detect mamlambo more reliably than Kin do. A Warg pack that senses a mamlambo will refuse the crossing and communicate the danger to any allied Kin or goblins.

### Society

Mamlambo are solitary river-predators of the steppe-river border — Huge, serpentine, and terrifyingly patient ambush hunters that claim river crossings as hunting-grounds. They are among the most feared natural predators of the Eternal Steppe's river margins, not because of their raw power (there are more powerful aquatic creatures in Oreka) but because of their method: the brain drain leaves corpses without faces or brains, which is deeply disturbing to every Kin culture and produces fear disproportionate to the creature's actual danger.

Warg packs take mamlambo more seriously than almost any other natural predator. The Harmony gives Wargs reliable detection of mamlambo presence, and Warg territorial knowledge includes detailed maps of which crossings are safe and which are claimed. A Warg pack that discovers a mamlambo at a previously-safe crossing communicates this information to every other pack in the region within days — the Warg intelligence network is faster and more reliable than any Kin communication system for this specific threat.

Mytroan steppe-riders rely on Warg intelligence for mamlambo avoidance. The Mytroan-Warg relationship, while complex politically, is unambiguous on this point: if the Wargs say a crossing has teeth, the Mytroans go around. Mytroan caravans carry "crossing-stones" — small river-pebbles enchanted by hedge-wise women to glow faintly warm when a corrupted water-signature is within 100 feet. The enchantment is unreliable (works approximately 60% of the time), but it provides a degree of reassurance at unfamiliar fords.

Eruskan river-traders encounter mamlambo only at the steppe-river border, where the Great River's tributaries run wide and slow. The Eruskan response to a confirmed mamlambo is practical: hire Mytroan riders and Warg scouts to locate the creature, then use coordinated ranged attacks from the bank to drive it downstream into territory where it is someone else's problem.

### Lore

**DC 18 (Knowledge [arcana]):** Mamlambo are Huge river-serpents that lie camouflaged on riverbeds and ambush wading creatures. Their bite drains Intelligence and consumes the victim's face and brain. They do not fight on land.

**DC 23:** A mamlambo's Hide bonus while submerged is extraordinary (+16 in riverbed). Warg packs detect them more reliably than Kin through the Harmony and share crossing-intelligence across the steppe. Mytroan "crossing-stones" glow near corrupted water-resonance but are unreliable.

**DC 28:** Mamlambo resonance reads as corrupted water — stagnant and predatory — partially masked by flowing water (DC 18 to detect). A mamlambo claims a single crossing and never leaves it. The faceless corpses left by brain drain have produced deep cultural horror across all steppe cultures.

### Combat

A manananggal hunts at night. Before sunset, it separates — the upper half detaching at the waist and sprouting bat-like wings — and takes flight, leaving the lower half hidden in a secure location. The flying upper body hunts sleeping or isolated victims, using its elongated, tubular tongue to drain blood through windows, tent-flaps, or other openings. It prefers to feed without waking its victim (Hide +10, Move Silently +10).

If discovered, the manananggal uses its horrifying visage to panic opponents, then attacks with claws and tongue. It retreats toward its lower half if reduced below 15 hit points — it must rejoin before dawn or suffer destruction.

### Special Abilities

**Separation (Su):** At dusk, a manananggal can separate its body at the waist as a full-round action. The upper half gains a fly speed of 40 ft. (good) and retains all combat abilities. The lower half is inert, helpless, and has AC 5. The upper half must rejoin the lower half before sunrise or be destroyed (see Vulnerability). While separated, the manananggal can sense the direction and distance to its lower half at any range.

### Society

Manananggal are Silence Breach undead — a specific category of undead that arise only in areas where the metaphysical damage from the Dómnathar's entry into Oreka has weakened the boundary between life and death. They are not Dómnathar creations (the Dómnathar do not create undead — their aesthetic is flesh-warping, not necromancy), but they are consequences of the Dómnathar presence.

A manananggal was always a living person — typically a woman — who died of violence in a Silence Breach zone and rose as this specific form of undead due to the corrupted metaphysical conditions. The transformation preserves intelligence and memory but inverts the personality: a kind woman becomes a cruel predator, a shy woman becomes a brazen hunter.

Hasura border-scouts in the Gatefall Reach consider manananggal one of the Breach's signature threats. The Hasura anti-manananggal protocol is specific: find the lower half before dawn. Hasura scouts patrol known Breach zones at dusk, watching for the separation-flight, then triangulate the direction of the lower half from the upper half's flight-path. Destroying the lower half (vinegar, salt, fire) permanently eliminates the creature. The Hasura maintain stocks of vinegar at every border-post specifically for this purpose.

Pasua ground-elves share the Hasura protocols. Eruskan communities near the Kinsweave Breach zones have adopted a simpler approach: garlic, salt, and thorn-vine barriers around windows and doorways at night (the tongue cannot penetrate thorn-vine, and garlic's strong scent masks the smell of sleeping victims).

### Lore

**DC 15 (Knowledge [religion]):** Manananggal are undead that split at the waist — the upper half flies and hunts, the lower half hides. They drain blood with an elongated tongue and must rejoin before dawn. Destroying the lower half destroys the creature permanently.

Vestri miners encounter monstrous spiders at every depth and consider them a routine hazard. Vestri mining-kit includes a short knife specifically designed for cutting web-silk.

Dómnathar tunnel-networks host monstrous spider populations that the Deceivers tolerate or cultivate — House Kethloryn (the spider-prophecy house) actively breeds Gargantuan specimens as tunnel-guardians and living web-barriers.

### Lore

**DC 11 (Knowledge [nature]):** Monstrous spiders are venomous predators found in every environment in Oreka. They range from Medium to Gargantuan. Web-spinners trap prey in adhesive webs; hunters stalk by tremorsense.

**DC 16:** Spider venom causes Strength damage. Webs are flammable (fire is the fastest counter-measure). Pasua rangers identify individual mega-spiders by web-pattern.

**DC 21:** House Kethloryn of the Dómnathar breeds Gargantuan spiders as tunnel-guardians. Monstrous spiders are functionally invisible to passive Kin-sense — their warm static is too faint to detect beyond 15 feet.

# MUD MENDERS

The riverbank is repairing itself. Where the spring flood tore a section away, leaving raw earth and exposed roots, something is working — dozens of small, wet, vaguely humanoid shapes made of river-mud and clay, moving with the busy purposefulness of ants. They are packing mud into the wound, weaving roots through the fill, and smoothing the surface with hands that are more suggestion than anatomy. One of them notices you watching. It stops, tilts its featureless head, and extends a hand that drips with silt — an offer to help, or a request that you not step on the work.

### Combat

Mud menders do not seek combat. They defend their work-sites if attacked, fighting with slow, deliberate slam attacks that coat targets in clinging mud. Their primary defensive strategy is mudslick — coating the ground around them in slippery mud that makes approach difficult — while they retreat toward the nearest body of water, where they dissolve into the mud and reform elsewhere.

### Special Abilities

**Mudslick (Ex):** A creature hit by a mud mender's slam is coated in clinging mud. The target must succeed on a DC 14 Reflex save or be slowed (as the *slow* spell) for 1d4 rounds as the mud hardens and restricts movement. The save DC is Constitution-based. Additionally, the ground within 10 feet of a mud mender is always slippery (Balance DC 14 to avoid falling prone when moving through).

**Mend Terrain (Su):** Mud menders can repair damaged terrain — eroded riverbanks, collapsed levees, silted channels, damaged marsh-ecosystems — at a rate of 10 cubic feet per hour per mender. A work-crew of eight can rebuild a collapsed riverbank in a single night. This is not magical construction — it is elemental earth-water manipulation, packing and shaping natural materials with supernatural precision. The repaired terrain is indistinguishable from natural formation.

**Earth Glide (Ex):** A mud mender can move through mud, silt, and wet earth as easily as a fish moves through water. It can use this ability to disappear into a riverbank or mudflat in a single round.

**Water-Earth Resonance (Gentle) (Su):** Mud menders register on Kin-sense as a soft, warm water-earth signature — gentle and industrious, like feeling a garden grow. Detectable at 30 feet. The resonance is the most benign signature of any elemental in Oreka — Kin describe it as "comforting," and halfling marsh-communities consider the presence of mud menders to be a sign of a healthy ecosystem.

### Society

Mud menders are the smallest and most benign elemental beings in Oreka — spontaneously-generated water-earth elementals that exist to repair and maintain the natural infrastructure of marshes, riverbanks, and tidal zones. They are not summoned, not created, and not controlled — they simply appear where the intersection of water and earth elemental energy is strong and the terrain needs mending.

Halfling communities of the Deepwater Marches and Tidebloom Reach consider mud menders essential partners in their wetland way of life. Halfling levee-engineers work alongside mud mender colonies, combining halfling architectural knowledge with the elementals' supernatural ability to shape mud and clay. The relationship is not formal (mud menders are too simple for negotiation) but it is deeply functional — halflings provide the design, mud menders provide the labour, and the result is wetland infrastructure that is more durable than either could produce alone.

Eruskan river-communities along the Great River's flood-plains value mud menders for the same reasons. Eruskan farmers consider a mud mender colony on their riverbank to be a blessing — the elementals repair flood-damage faster than human work-crews and do it for free.

Eruskan naturalists classify mud menders as "constructive elementals" — a rare category of elemental being that builds rather than destroys.

### Lore

**DC 13 (Knowledge [the planes]):** Mud menders are Small water-earth elementals that repair riverbanks, levees, and marsh terrain. They are not aggressive and fight only in self-defence. They dissolve into mud when threatened.

**DC 18:** Mud mender work-crews can rebuild a collapsed riverbank in a single night. Their mud-slick ability slows opponents in clinging mud. Halfling wetland-communities work alongside them as construction partners.

**DC 23:** Mud menders generate spontaneously where water-earth elemental energy is concentrated. Their gentle resonance is the most benign elemental signature in Oreka — halflings consider their presence a sign of ecosystem health. They can earth-glide through wet soil at full speed.

# MUMMY

The sarcophagus is not dwarven. It is Giant — carved from a single block of basalt in proportions that no Kin hands shaped, covered in rune-script that predates written Dwarven by millennia. The lid has been moved. From inside, the smell of preservation-resin and dust and something else — something dry and electric that makes the hair on your arms stand up. The thing that climbs out does not rush. It has been waiting for longer than your civilisation has existed, and it has the patience of stone. The wrappings are not cloth — they are strips of treated hide, each inscribed with runes that glow faintly in the dark. Its eyes are empty hollows that see you anyway.

### Combat

A mummy is slow, powerful, and terrifying. It opens engagement with its despair ability — a supernatural aura of dread that paralyzes weaker-willed opponents — then closes and hammers with its slam. Every blow carries mummy rot, a wasting disease that defies normal healing. A mummy does not chase fleeing opponents beyond its tomb — it returns to its sarcophagus and waits.

### Special Abilities

**Despair (Su):** At the sight of a mummy, creatures within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 16 Will save or be paralyzed with fear for 1d4 rounds. Whether or not the save is successful, the creature is immune to that mummy's despair for 24 hours. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Mummy Rot (Su):** Slam — supernatural disease; Fortitude DC 16; incubation 1 minute; damage 1d6 Con and 1d6 Cha. Unlike normal diseases, mummy rot continues until the victim reaches Constitution 0 (death) or the disease is removed. *Remove disease* does not cure mummy rot — it requires *remove curse* followed by *remove disease* (in that order). An afflicted creature that dies of mummy rot crumbles to dust and cannot be raised without *resurrection* or more powerful magic. The save DC is Charisma-based.

**Undead-Giant Resonance (Su):** A mummy registers on Kin-sense as a distorted undead signature with a faint, fragmentary echo of Giant four-element resonance — the all-element harmony that the Giants once possessed, now frozen in the preservation-runes and slowly degrading. The effect is deeply unsettling: Kin describe it as "hearing the last note of a song that ended ten thousand years ago, played backwards." Detectable at 60 feet (no check required). Pekakarlik pilgrims who encounter mummies in Giant tombs report that the resonance provokes grief as much as fear — these were once the greatest beings in Oreka, and their undead remnants are a desecration of what they were.

### Society

Mummies in Oreka are exclusively Giant-era undead — the preserved remains of Giant servants, retainers, and (rarely) Giants themselves, bound to their tombs by preservation-runes that were intended to honour the dead but instead trapped them between death and dissolution. They are not created through necromancy — they are the product of Giant funerary magic interacting with millennia of elemental decay.

Pekakarlik dwarves, whose ruin-pilgrimages frequently lead through Giant tombs, treat mummies with a mixture of reverence and caution. A mummy is not an enemy to a Pekakarlik — it is a desecrated sacred thing, a Giant-era tragedy that should be laid to rest. Pekakarlik tomb-teams carry consecrated oils and *remove curse* scrolls specifically for the purpose of releasing mummies from their binding — not destroying them, but freeing them. A Pekakarlik who destroys a mummy without attempting release first is considered to have acted dishonourably.

Eruskan tomb-scholars study mummy-wrappings for the rune-script they carry — these are among the oldest surviving examples of Giant written language, and a single mummy's wrappings may contain historical information found nowhere else.

### Lore

**DC 15 (Knowledge [religion]):** Mummies are undead found in Giant tombs. Their slam carries mummy rot — a wasting disease that requires *remove curse* before *remove disease*. Their appearance causes paralysing dread. They are vulnerable to fire.

**DC 20:** Mummy rot kills if untreated and prevents resurrection below the *resurrection* spell level. Mummies have DR 5/— and +4 turn resistance. Pekakarlik tomb-teams carry consecrated oils to release mummies rather than destroy them.

**DC 25:** Oreka's mummies are exclusively Giant-era — products of funerary magic, not necromancy. Their resonance carries a fragmentary echo of Giant four-element harmony. The rune-script on their wrappings is among the oldest surviving Giant writing — of immense scholarly value.

# MUSHUSSU

It has the body of a scaled serpent, the forelegs of a lion, the hind-legs of an eagle, and a neck that arches upward like a crane's, capped with a horned head whose eyes glow with a faint, steady amber light. It has been sitting in front of this gate for ten thousand years. The bones at its feet suggest that it takes its duties seriously.

### Combat

A mushussu fights as a gate-guardian — it does not pursue, does not range, and does not leave its post. It challenges any creature that approaches its designated gate or entrance, and attacks anything that attempts to pass without permission (how permission is granted varies — some mushussu respond to Giant-language commands, others to specific rune-tokens, and some allow passage to any creature that makes a formal request and waits). In combat, it opens with its breath weapon to scatter groups, then engages the closest target with bite and claws. Its venom compounds the damage.

A mushussu fights to the death. It has no retreat behaviour. It has been guarding this gate for ten thousand years, and it will guard it until it is destroyed.

### Special Abilities

**Breath Weapon (Su):** 30-foot cone of searing, elemental-charged wind, usable every 1d4 rounds. 4d6 damage (half fire, half electricity). Reflex DC 19 half. The save DC is Constitution-based. The breath carries the faint, painful resonance of Giant-era elemental energy — Kin who fail the save are dazed for 1 round by the resonance-shock in addition to the damage.

**Poison (Ex):** Bite; Fortitude DC 19; initial damage 1d4 Str; secondary damage 1d4 Con. The venom is a thick, oily substance that smells of heated metal. The save DC is Constitution-based.

**Ruin-Sentinel (Su):** A mushussu bonded to a specific gate or entrance cannot be moved from its post by any forced-movement effect (bull rush, grapple repositioning, teleportation). It receives a +4 bonus on saves against enchantment effects while within 60 feet of its bonded site. If killed, a mushussu reforms at its bonded gate in 1d4 months — only *wish* or *miracle* can permanently destroy a gate-bonded mushussu.

**Fading All-Element Resonance (Su):** A mushussu registers on Kin-sense as a fading echo of the all-four-element resonance — the same harmony that lamassu carry, but weaker and flickering, like a candle in its last hours of burning. Pekakarlik ruin-wardens describe it as "the gate itself remembering that it was alive." Detectable at 90 feet. Unlike lamassu resonance (overwhelming and sustained), mushussu resonance is melancholy — Kin who detect it report a sense of endings, of duty without purpose, of a guard that no longer knows what it guards.

### Society

Mushussu are the last gate-guardians of the Giant cities — composite magical beasts (serpent, lion, eagle) created or adopted by the Giants as sentinels for their most important structures. They predate the Kin; they predate the Shattering; they predate everything except the Giants themselves. Each mushussu is bonded to a specific gate, and it has been guarding that gate since the Giant who placed it there crumbled to dust.

Pekakarlik ruin-wardens maintain careful records of known mushussu locations, their temperaments, and the methods (if any) by which they grant passage. Some mushussu respond to spoken Giant-language — a Pekakarlik who has learned the Giant tongue from lamassu or from rune-study can request passage formally. Others respond to specific rune-tokens that the Pekakarlik have painstakingly recreated from Giant inscription-fragments. And some respond to nothing at all — they guard gates to structures that have been empty for millennia and will guard them until the world ends.

The relationship between mushussu and lamassu is unclear. Lamassu are intelligent, conversational, and willing to answer questions; mushussu are not (Int 6 — they understand commands but do not converse). Whether mushussu were created by the Giants as lesser guardians, or evolved independently and were adopted by the Giants, is debated.

Taraf-Imro warriors who have fought mushussu at ruin-entrances describe them as the most relentless opponents they have faced — a mushussu does not tire, does not retreat, and reforms after death.

### Lore

**DC 18 (Knowledge [arcana]):** Mushussu are composite magical beasts — serpent, lion, and eagle — that guard the gates of Giant ruins. They have a breath weapon (fire/electricity cone), venomous bite, and spell resistance 18. They fight to the death and do not leave their post.

**DC 23:** A mushussu bonded to a gate reforms in 1d4 months after destruction — only *wish* can prevent this. Pekakarlik ruin-wardens maintain records of each mushussu's temperament and the methods for requesting passage. Some respond to Giant-language commands.

**DC 28:** Mushussu carry a fading echo of Giant four-element resonance — weaker and more melancholy than lamassu resonance. They predate the Kin entirely. The resonance provokes a sense of endings in Kin who detect it — "the gate itself remembering that it was alive."
