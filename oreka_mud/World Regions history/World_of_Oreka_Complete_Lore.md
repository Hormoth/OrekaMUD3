## Overview

**Oreka** is a continent-spanning world shaped by elemental forces, divine creation, and centuries of war, loss, and rebuilding. Its history follows a grand arc: the **Elemental Lords** created the world; the **Giants**, the Firstborn Kin, raised the first civilizations; successive races -- **Elves**, **Dwarves**, **Halflings**, and **Humans** -- inherited and expanded upon the Giants' legacy under the unity of the **Eke Concordant**. That unity shattered when the Third Crowned Keeper failed, fracturing humanity into four warring subraces in the event known as the **Shattering of Man**. Centuries later, the **Deceivers' War** brought an extraplanar invasion through the **Silence Breach**, devastating the Kinsweave and Tidebloom, annihilating the Aldenheim Dwarves, and scattering hostile remnants across the south. From the ashes of that war rose the **Ascended Gods** -- mortal Kin who achieved apotheosis through extraordinary sacrifice -- and a changed world where old grudges, new peoples, and ancient ruins define every region.

The world is bound together by **Kin-sense**, an elemental resonance shared by all true Kin that allows them to feel each other's presence, and by the four elements -- **Stone**, **Fire**, **Water**, and **Wind** -- that shape every race, culture, and magical tradition. Five major regions anchor the setting: the guilt-scarred **Kinsweave** in the north-central highlands, the frontier wilderness of the **Deepwater Marches** in the southwest, the wind-swept **Eternal Steppe** of the south-central plains, the ancient and pilgrimage-haunted **Infinite Desert** along the equatorial belt, and the resilient coastal **Tidebloom Reach** in the northeast.

## The Kinsweave

**Settlements of North Central Oreka**

> "Six thrones stood empty by morning. The Golden Rose had visited each in the night, and she left nothing behind but cold crowns and the smell of wilting flowers."
> -- from the *Lament of Kings*, author unknown

### Kinsweave Regional History

The **Kinsweave** is a region defined by what it lost. Once part of the **Eke Concordant's** unified human governance under the Giants and Keepers, North Central Oreka was home to a constellation of human kingdoms whose cities straddled the **Giant's Teeth** mountains, the **Great River's** upper reaches, and the volcanic **Scorchspires**. When the Concordant held, this was prosperous country -- Orean quarries supplied stone for half the continent's fortifications, Eruskan river-traders connected the inland settlements to the wider world, and Taraf-Imro forges armed the Keepers' legions.

The **Shattering of Man** broke it all apart. Each of the four human Kin blame the others for the catastrophe, and in the Kinsweave the scars run especially deep. The region's kingdoms fractured along subrace lines, old trade partnerships curdled into suspicion, and the unity that had defined the Concordant era gave way to bitter insularity. The Eruskan port cities turned inward, guarding their river commerce jealously. The Orean mountain settlements fortified and stopped trusting anyone who did not smell of stone dust. The Taraf-Imro, always passionate, channeled their fury into martial ambition and forge-craft, nursing grudges that still burn hot three centuries later.

Then came the **Deceivers' War**, and with it the Kinsweave's deepest shame. When the **Aldenheim Dwarves** sent word that the Deceiver host was bearing down upon them and begged for aid, the kings of the Kinsweave's minor kingdoms refused. Each had his excuse -- insufficient men, stolen glory, profit to protect, a war that "wasn't their fight" -- but the result was the same. Aldenheim fell. And when **Harreem, the Golden Rose**, rose to godhood on a tide of retributive fury, she summoned all twelve human monarchs who had refused the call to her father's court at Pryee and judged them together in a single hall. By morning, all twelve thrones stood empty. The poem known as the *Lament of Kings* preserves their excuses and their fates, and it is recited every year at the blasted plain that sits atop the flattened ruins of Aldenheim.

Six of those twelve kingdoms lay within the Kinsweave: **Mytro**, **Kalite**, **Hylen**, **Thrush**, **Andrio**, and **Pryee**. The Border Kingdoms -- Pryee, Andrio, and Thrush -- were among the Twelve Kingdoms and share in the shame of the kings' refusal. Their cities are ruins now, haunted by **Sand Wraiths** and Deceiver artifacts, monuments to the cost of cowardice. Five cities survived: **Stonefall**, **Rivertop**, **Highridge**, **Lakeshore**, and **Lakewell**. These are the living cities of the Kinsweave, and they carry the weight of their region's history in every festival, every prayer, and every sidelong glance between subraces who remember what the Shattering cost them.

Nearly all of the **Ascended Gods** are actively worshipped across the Kinsweave, reflecting the region's proximity to the Lament of Kings site and the deep theological significance of apotheosis in a land scarred by divine retribution. Harreem's shadow falls long here, and the living cities navigate a complex spiritual landscape where the gods who rose at Aldenheim are both sources of comfort and reminders of catastrophic failure.

### Kinsweave Landscape and Geography

North Central Oreka is a region of rugged mountains, river valleys, and volcanic zones. The **Giant's Teeth** dominate the north, their peaks still bearing the rune-stamped stonework of the Giants who shaped them in ages past. The **Great River** flows from the mountains through the region's heart, feeding the twin lakes that give Lakeshore and Lakewell their livelihoods. To the southeast, the **Scorchspires** rise in a jagged line of volcanic peaks where lava vents warm the air year-round and the Lady of Fire's influence saturates the stone.

The land between these anchors is mixed country -- rolling foothills, scrubby grassland, and river-cut valleys where farming settlements cling to fertile bottomland. The ruins of the six fallen kingdoms are scattered across this middle ground, their crumbling walls and sand-choked streets a constant reminder that civilization here is fragile. Sandstorms blow through the ruined zones with punishing regularity, and the environmental hazards of the wastelands demand preparation from any traveller foolish enough to cross them without a guide.

### Kinsweave Living Settlements

#### Stonefall -- Orean Bastion of the Giant's Teeth

**Population:** ~10,000
**Demographics:** Vast majority Orean humans, significant Visetri Dwarf minority, scattering of Hasura Elves and Halflings

Living in Stonefall feels like being cradled by the mountains. The city's sturdy stone halls and watchtowers are carved directly into the Giant's Teeth, offering security against tremors and raiders alike. The streets hum with the rhythm of chisels and the aroma of barley bread, while runestone markers guide residents through the dusty air. Oreans are steadfast people who value tradition and community, though they remain wary of outsiders -- particularly Mytroan nomads, whose wandering ways sit uneasily with Stonefall's deep-rooted sense of place.

The city was founded during the Eke Concordant as a farming and quarrying hub, growing under the Keepers' guidance into a centre of basalt extraction and stonecraft. It weathered the Shattering by fortifying its valleys, becoming a symbol of resilience but also of insularity. Spared the worst of the Deceivers' War, Stonefall honours the fallen Aldenheim Dwarves with a shrine to **Ludus Galerius** near Tharok's pillar, reflecting its quiet pride in survival and its uneasy awareness of the debt it owes to the dead.

**Leadership:**
- **Elder Torv Graniteheart** -- Orean fighter of long experience and stoic temperament who personally led the reconstruction of Stonefall's walls after the Shattering
- **Hilda Earthvein** -- druid who oversees the city's sacred stone circles
- **Drenna Rockbinder** -- Visetri dwarf who manages the quarry trade

**Key Locations:**
- **Granite Hold** -- central citadel reinforced with runestone defences, houses the Elder's council
- **Quarrymarket** -- vibrant trade square for basalt blocks, stone tools, and runestone artifacts
- **Earthcircle Grove** -- sacred druidic site hosting stone shape rituals honouring the Lord of Stone
- **Watchtower Rise** -- defensive oversight across surrounding valleys

**Festival:** The annual **Stonehearth Festival** celebrates the Lord of Stone with stone-carving contests and communal feasts, drawing artisans and traders from across the region.

**Principal Exports:** Basalt blocks, stone tools, barley, root crops, runestone artifacts

#### Rivertop -- Eruskan Port of the Great River

**Population:** ~7,500
**Demographics:** Predominantly Eruskan humans, substantial Pekakarlik Dwarf community, lively Halfling minority

The city pulses with the energy of the Great River, its docks alive with mariners' chants and the creak of barges. Eruskans, with their characteristic sea-blue eyes, navigate the river's swift currents with practised ease, while Halfling taverns and Pekakarlik cliffside workshops add warmth and grit to the waterfront.

Rivertop flourished as an Eke Concordant trade hub under the Keepers until the Shattering's floods damaged its docks. Pekakarlik engineers rebuilt them stronger, cementing the partnership between the two peoples. Rivertop maintains neutrality toward the region's elven communities but remains wary of Taraf-Imro due to trade rivalries that hardened during the Deceivers' War.

**Leadership:**
- **Mayor Senna Wavecourt** -- shrewd Eruskan rogue who quelled the post-Shattering riots through cunning negotiation and well-timed bribes
- **Seryn Tidecaller** -- sorcerer who leads the city's water spirit ceremonies
- **Brix Deepcurrent** -- Pekakarlik ranger who oversees dock safety

**Key Locations:**
- **Wavefront Docks** -- bustling piers with rune-carved pilings where River Serpents (CR 4) occasionally test the guards' vigilance
- **Mistmarket** -- trades in fish, river-sourced textiles, and Riverstone Amulets (+5 Swim, water breathing 1/day)
- **Seaspray Shrine** -- water spirit devotions
- **Tidewatch Tower** -- river hazard watch

**Festival:** The annual **Riverflow Festival** honours the Lady of the Sea with boat races and mist-summoning rituals, as glowing lanterns float downriver.

**Principal Exports:** Fish, river-sourced textiles, Riverstone Amulets, trade barges

#### Highridge -- Taraf-Imro Stronghold of the Scorchspires

**Population:** ~6,000
**Demographics:** Three-quarters Taraf-Imro humans, meaningful Kovaka Elf presence, Rarozhki Dwarf community

A fiery stronghold where the glow of forges and the heat of volcanic vents shape daily life. Taraf-Imro warriors, with their red hair and amber eyes, craft embersteel weapons in cliffside smithies, their ambition and passion fuelling both creation and conflict. Kovaka Elves bring earthy resilience, while Rarozhki Dwarves forge alongside the humans -- though past betrayals breed a tension that surfaces whenever the ale flows freely.

Highridge emerged during the Eke Concordant as a Taraf-Imro outpost, its forges vital to the human war machine. The Shattering's lava flows scarred the city and deepened distrust from Rarozhki and Eruskan neighbours. Its failure to aid the Aldenheim Dwarves left a stain on its honour that fuels its drive for redemption through martial prowess to this day.

**Leadership:**
- **Chieftain Ignar Flameborn** -- glory-seeking smith and fighter who forged an Embersteel Blade to repel raiders
- **Feryn Emberstrike** -- Taraf-Imro sorcerer who channels fire magic
- **Kharaz Fireforge** -- Rarozhki forge mystic who crafts sacred embersteel

**Key Locations:**
- **The Emberforge** -- massive cliffside smithy producing Embersteel Blades (+1 enhancement, 1d6 fire damage on hit)
- **Scorchmarket** -- trades in weapons and volcanic gems under the watchful eyes of Ember Drakes (CR 3, breath weapon 2d6 fire, Reflex DC 13)
- **Flameshrine** -- temple to the Lady of Fire, amplifies fire spells cast within its walls
- **Lavaflow Bastion** -- defensive walls reinforced with molten traps (Reflex DC 15, 2d6 fire damage)

**Festival:** The annual **Forgeflame Festival** honours the Lady of Fire with synchronised hammer strikes and fire-dancing.

**Principal Exports:** Embersteel weapons, volcanic gems, forged tools, fire-powders

#### Lakeshore -- Eruskan Port of the Great Lake

**Population:** ~5,500
**Demographics:** Predominantly Eruskan humans, lively Halfling community, smaller Pekakarlik Dwarf population

A serene port city where Eruskan mariners glide across a tranquil lake feeding the Great River. Wooden docks and stilted homes reflect the Eruskan's fluid grace, with water spirit shrines lining the shore. The city's calm exterior hides a deep mistrust of Taraf-Imro rooted in the Shattering's resource wars.

**Leadership:**
- **Mayor Kail Seacrest** -- cunning Eruskan navigator and rogue who secured the city's trade routes
- **Marin Wavewhisper** -- cleric of the Lady of the Sea who leads lake rituals
- **Drenna Streamcarver** -- Pekakarlik boat-builder

**Key Locations:**
- **Lakedock Plaza** -- trade hub for fish and boats, Riverstone Amulets available
- **Waveshrine** -- enhances water magic
- **Stiltmarket** -- Halfling merchants in elevated stalls
- **Seaguard Spire** -- lake hazard watch

**Festival:** The annual **Lakebloom Festival** features boat parades and water-summoning rites with glowing lanterns and communal feasts.

**Principal Exports:** Fish, riverboats, Riverstone Amulets, lake pearls

#### Lakewell -- Eruskan Haven of the Lesser Lake

**Population:** ~4,000
**Demographics:** Predominantly Eruskan humans, Pekakarlik Dwarves, Halflings

The smallest of the Kinsweave's living cities. A quieter counterpart to Lakeshore, it is a compact port city on a smaller lake tied to the Great River. Reed-roofed homes and floating markets embody the Lady of the Sea's playful spirit.

Founded as an Eke Concordant fishing village, Lakewell grew with Pekakarlik boat-building expertise. The Shattering's floods forced a relocation to higher ground, but Halfling optimism and Eruskan resilience rebuilt the city stronger.

**Leadership:**
- **Mayor Torin Saltfurrow** -- seasoned Eruskan trader and former river captain
- **Nessa Streamknot** -- Halfling rogue who runs the floating markets
- **Mosset Tidebrace** -- Pekakarlik ranger who guards the docks

**Key Locations:**
- **Floatmarket** -- network of rafted stalls trading in nets and pearls
- **Reedtemple** -- amplifies water magic
- **Boatwright Quay** -- Pekakarlik boat-building, produces River Spears
- **Lakemist Spire** -- river threat watch

**Festival:** The annual **Reedwave Festival** celebrates water spirits with net-weaving contests and mist-dances.

**Principal Exports:** Fish, woven nets, riverboats, lake pearls

### The Ruined Kingdoms

The ruins of the six fallen kingdoms -- **Mytro**, **Kalite**, **Hylen**, **Thrush**, **Andrio**, and **Pryee** -- lie scattered across the Kinsweave's middle ground, remnants of the Eke Concordant's minor kingdoms and Border Kingdoms. All were part of the Twelve Kingdoms, and all share in the shame of the kings' refusal to aid Aldenheim. Punished by **Harreem, the Golden Rose**, who assassinated their twelve monarchs for their betrayal, these ruins are haunted by **Sand Wraiths** (CR 5, desiccating touch 1d6 + 1d4 Constitution damage, Fortitude DC 14) and hold Deceiver artifacts that make them perilous adventuring sites.

- **Mytro** -- Mytroan nomadic hub on the plains' edge, tent foundations buried under sand. Its king claimed the war "wasn't their fight." The Mytrone migrated south, leaving a ghost town.
- **Kalite** -- Eruskan port on the Great River, now silted and choked. Sand Wraiths guard lost Riverstone Amulets. Its king refused aid to avoid "stolen glory."
- **Hylen** -- Mytroan windswept outpost whose king cited insufficient men. Storm Wisps (CR 2, electric touch 1d4) still linger among the shredded tent-poles.
- **Thrush** -- Taraf-Imro forge-town near the Scorchspires, cold smelters guarded by Ember Drakes (CR 3, breath weapon 2d6 fire, Reflex DC 13). Its king chose profit over honour.
- **Andrio** -- Mixed Taraf-Imro and Orean volcanic valley city whose king called aid "folly." Crumbling basalt halls sit atop Giant ruins (Disable Device DC 20).
- **Pryee** -- Taraf-Imro and Orean fiery citadel whose king dismissed the war entirely. Lava tubes now house Sand Wraiths.

Centred around the **Lament of Kings** site -- the blasted plain atop the flattened ruins of Aldenheim -- these ruins stand as monuments to the cost of cowardice. The annual Lament of Kings festival draws Kin from across Oreka to honour the fallen, gathering at the **Tomb of Kings** stage. The ruins remain dangerous outside the festival grounds, with sandstorms (Survival DC 15 to navigate, 1d4 damage per hour of exposure) and Deceiver relics posing constant threats.

### Kinsweave Adventure Hooks

**The Runestone Speaks.** A newly excavated runestone in Stonefall's Earthcircle Grove has begun emitting a low hum that Hilda Earthvein cannot explain. The resonance matches no known elemental frequency, and Elder Graniteheart wants it investigated before the Stonehearth Festival draws crowds. The stone may be a Giant-era warning system reactivating -- but warning of what?

**Blood on the River.** Eruskan barges between Rivertop and Lakeshore have been found drifting empty, their crews vanished without signs of struggle. Mayor Wavecourt suspects Taraf-Imro sabotage; Chieftain Flameborn calls the accusation slander. The truth may lie beneath the river's surface, where something older than either city has begun to stir.

**The Forge That Won't Cool.** Highridge's Emberforge has been burning hotter than any natural vent can explain. Kharaz Fireforge believes a Deceiver artifact buried in the lava tubes beneath the city is feeding the flames. Reaching it means descending into passages that have not been walked since the Deceivers' War -- and whatever guards the artifact has had three centuries to prepare.

**The Lament Walks.** During the most recent Lament of Kings festival, a pilgrim claims to have seen one of the dead kings walking the ruins of Kalite at dawn. The Golden Roses dismiss it as grief-madness, but a second witness has come forward, and the description matches the historical accounts of King Verith of Kalite. If the dead are not resting, the question is whether this is necromancy, haunting, or something worse.

**The Floatmarket Forgery.** Lakewell's Nessa Streamknot has discovered counterfeit Riverstone Amulets circulating through the Floatmarket -- convincing fakes that provide no magical benefit. The trail leads upriver to Rivertop, where the implications threaten the trade relationship between the two cities.

### Notes on the Peoples of the Kinsweave

The Kinsweave is predominantly human, and the tensions between the four human subraces define the region's character more than any other single factor. **Oreans** are stoic, community-minded builders with a deep Earth-element affinity and a tendency toward insularity. **Eruskans** are fluid, trade-minded mariners attuned to Water, pragmatic and adaptable but distrustful of those who threaten their commerce. **Taraf-Imro** are passionate, glory-driven warriors with Fire-element attunement, whose ambition and pride make them both inspiring allies and dangerous neighbours. The **Mytrone** -- those who remain -- are nomadic Wind-attuned horsemen whose wandering ways sit uneasily with the settled cities.

The **Visetri Dwarves** maintain a meaningful presence in Stonefall, complementing the Oreans' stonecraft. **Pekakarlik Dwarves** are essential to the river cities, their Water-element affinity keeping docks, canals, and flood defences in working order. **Rarozhki Dwarves** share Highridge's forges with the Taraf-Imro in a partnership defined by mutual respect and lingering grievance. **Kovaka Elves** bring Earth-element resilience to the mountain settlements, while **Hasura Elves** maintain a scattering of communities.

When two Kin of different races have a child, that child is born as one race or the other -- never a hybrid. A Pekakarlik Dwarf and a Human will produce either a Pekakarlik child or a Human child. This is a fundamental property of the elemental framework: Kin-sense and elemental identity are indivisible, not blendable. Mixed-race families are not uncommon in the Kinsweave.

### Kinsweave Named Land Features

- **The Giant's Teeth** -- prominent mountain range in the north, natural fortress and monument to the fallen Giants
- **The Great River** -- flows from the mountains through the region's heart, feeding the twin lakes and supporting river commerce
- **The Scorchspires** -- jagged line of volcanic peaks to the southeast, defining Highridge's character
- **The Lament of Kings** -- blasted plain atop the ruins of Aldenheim, the region's spiritual and historical anchor

### Kinsweave Campaign Integration

The Kinsweave offers rich campaign opportunities blending elemental themes, cultural tensions, and historical scars. Stonefall is ideal for earth magic and Giant lore quests (Knowledge [History] DC 20). Rivertop and Lakewell suit water-based adventures with trade disputes (Diplomacy DC 20) or River Serpents. Lakeshore serves as a hub for social and exploration encounters. Highridge is perfect for combat-heavy campaigns with forge espionage or Deceiver artifact recovery.

The ruined kingdoms offer high-risk, high-reward adventuring with Sand Wraiths, traps (Disable Device DC 20), and Deceiver artifacts. The **Sand Warden** prestige class suits Pekakarlik Dwarves and Orean Humans. The Lament of Kings festival provides social encounters that unite Kin but spark tensions (Diplomacy DC 20), and the interplay between living cities and dead kingdoms creates a natural campaign arc from local intrigue to region-shaping revelation.

## The Deepwater Marches

### Deepwater Marches History

#### The Giant's Legacy and the Apelian Sea

The southwest of Oreka was shaped by water long before any Kin walked its shores. The **Apelian Sea** -- the great southern inland mega-lake -- filled a vast basin south of the Infinite Desert, its warm, sediment-rich waters fed by underground channels that the Giants themselves had carved to balance the world's hydrology. Unlike the cold, storm-wracked **Kailian Sea** in the north, the Apelian is warm year-round, its shores thick with mangrove and jungle growth. The **Sea Falls** connect the two mega-lakes through the body of Oreka, and in the Giant era, this waterway was the spine of continental trade.

The Giants built along the Apelian's northern shore and western rim -- cities of dark stone veined with green mineral, roofed with living canopy, and designed to channel the warm rains. Rune-circles stabilized the lakeside soil, and great stone piers extended into the shallows. Today those piers are submerged, and the rune-circles have been swallowed by root systems so ancient that the stones have become indistinguishable from the jungle floor.

#### The Eke Concordant and the Southern Reach

When the Eke Concordant unified the Kin, the Deepwater Marches were the frontier -- the southernmost extent of organized Kin civilization. **Pasua Elves** settled the great forests south and west of the Apelian. Halflings established trading posts along the Sea Falls portage routes. **Kovaka Elves** carved stone outposts where the jungle met the mountain foothills. Even at its height, the Marches were never densely populated. The jungle was too thick, the terrain too difficult, and the distance from the northern centers of power too great.

#### The Shattering of Man

When the Third Crowned Keeper's failure shattered the Concordant, the Deepwater Marches felt the collapse as abandonment rather than destruction. The trade routes simply stopped. The Pekakarlik barges ceased their runs. The Pasua villages and Halfling stilt-towns adjusted -- but the adjustment was permanent. The Marches became a land cut off from the world above the desert, answerable to no authority.

#### The Deceivers' War and the Dark Dawn

The Deceivers' arrival sent shockwaves across southern Oreka, but the aftermath transformed the Deepwater Marches forever. The Deceiver host marched north through the Eternal Steppe and into the Infinite Desert, bypassing the Marches. The Kin of the south pursued the invaders and brought them to battle on the Steppe at the **Battle of Dark Dawn**.

When the Deceiver captains ordered the deliberate scatter, hundreds of warbands fled in every direction. Many headed west and southwest, into the deep jungles around the Apelian Sea. They brought goblin servitor-troops, war-wargs, hobgoblin officers, and alien equipment. The goblins and wargs were not content to remain tools. The chaos of Dark Dawn cracked the chains of Deceiver command. Within a generation, the Deepwater Marches transformed from a quiet Kin frontier to a contested wildland where Kin conclaves, free goblin tribes, wargbond communities, Deceiver remnants, and opportunistic predators all competed for territory.

#### The Farborn Arrive

Among the forces that fled the Deceiver host was the **Gilded Standard Company** -- approximately two thousand fighting men and four thousand camp followers from another world. When Commander **Captain Ralven Duskharrow** learned the Domnathar intended to use the Company as disposable shock troops, he refused. The Company broke away in a fighting withdrawal and fled south, settling at the **Lone Titan**. Over three centuries, they transformed into the people now known as the **Farborn**, growing to approximately twenty-eight thousand, with their legendary **Titanbreed** hybrid horses becoming the most coveted cavalry mounts on the continent.

#### The Present Day

Today the Deepwater Marches are the most contested and least governed region in Oreka -- a true frontier where power is held by strength, not by treaty. No single authority governs the Marches. The five major settlements maintain fragile trade agreements and mutual defense pacts, renegotiated constantly and enforced only by the threat of withdrawal.

The jungle itself is the region's truest ruler. **Wargbond** tribes control vast stretches of deep jungle as genuinely egalitarian societies. **Flamewarg cults** haunt the transitions between jungle and steppe. On the region's southern rim, the **Domnathar stronghold** has been quietly rebuilt over 300 years from the Deceiver remnant core. Nearby, the **Deceiver's Refuge** teaches the forbidden **Deceiver's Feat** -- concealing one's Kin-sense signature.

The region's total settled population stands at roughly 42,000 souls, but goblin and warg populations of the deep jungle likely exceed this by a significant margin.

### Deepwater Marches Landscape and Named Landmarks

The Deepwater Marches occupy the southwest of Oreka. The terrain is overwhelmingly forested -- dense tropical and subtropical jungle. The climate is warm year-round (80-95 degrees F), humid near the lake.

#### The Apelian Sea

The great southern freshwater mega-lake, warm and sediment-rich, deep blue-green. Shores are low, marshy, and thick with mangrove growth. Supports extraordinary aquatic diversity -- massive freshwater fish, water serpents (CR 4, Swim DC 15), and luminescent eels. Halfling stilt-villages and Pasua canoe-stations dot the western and northern shores.

#### The Deepwood

The vast tropical jungle south and west of the Apelian Sea. Canopy rises 150 to 200 feet in the oldest growth, blocking sunlight so completely that the forest floor exists in permanent twilight. Wargbond tribes and Free Goblin clans control much of the interior. The Deepwood is laced with Giant ruins -- dark stone foundations, submerged piers, and rune-circles so overgrown they appear to be natural rock formations.

#### The Lone Titan

A massive solitary mountain on the Marches' eastern edge. Its deep roots disrupt Domnathar scrying, creating a metaphysical "dead zone" that shields Titan's Rest from arcane detection. The mountain supports terraced farms and the Farborn's Titanbreed horse grazing lands. At the summit, a Giant-origin rune-circle pulses with faint amber light on clear nights.

#### The Verdant Shoals

The marshy western shore of the Apelian Sea -- a labyrinth of shallow channels, reed beds, and floating islands. Primary source of lake pearls, medicinal reeds, and luminescent eel-oil. Navigation requires Profession [Sailor] DC 15 or Knowledge [nature] DC 18.

#### The Spur

A rocky peninsula extending into the Apelian's southern shore, crowned by a Domnathar-origin tower -- the hidden stronghold of the Deepwater Remnant, quietly rebuilt over 300 years. Kin-sense flickers or goes dead within a mile of the structure. The **Deceiver's Refuge** sits at the base.

> **DM NOTE:** The Spur Tower IS the Domnathar hidden stronghold. The pre-invasion house leaders who fled here are still alive (Domnathar lifespan: up to 1,000 years) and have personal memory of both their home world and the full 300-year rebuilding effort. The Kin-sense dead zone is generated by worked Domnathar metal in the foundations. The relationship between the tower and the Deceiver's Refuge is left to the DM.

#### The Ash Trails

A network of burned paths through the Deepwood, originally created by Flamewarg cults. Over decades these have become the closest thing the Marches have to roads -- faster than cutting through virgin jungle (normal overland movement rather than half-speed) but predictable, making them prime ambush territory.

### Deepwater Marches Campaign Integration

The Deepwater Marches are the frontier where civilization's rules thin to nothing. **Tidewell** anchors water-and-trade arcs. **Canopy Hold** is ideal for exploration and survival campaigns. **Thornwall** provides distinctive roleplaying with goblin and warg PCs. **Titan's Rest** and the Farborn offer military and diplomatic arcs. **Murtavah Root** and the Deceiver's Refuge are the Marches' mystery engines.

The Marches' greatest narrative strength is moral ambiguity. There are no clear villains here -- even the Flamewarg cults are descendants of slaves, and even the Deceiver's Refuge teaches skills that save lives.

## The Eternal Steppe

**Where the Wind Remembers What Kings Forget**
*A Regional Guide for Oreka -- v4 -- D&D 3.5e Campaign Setting*

> "The Mytrone do not build walls because walls cannot follow the herd. They do not crown kings because a king who sits still is already dead. They ride, and the steppe rides with them."

### Eternal Steppe History

#### The Giant's Legacy and the Open Sky

The south-central expanse of Oreka was never a land the Giants chose to build upon. The great grasslands were left deliberately open -- a vast commons where the wind could run unbroken. What the Giants left were waymarks: enormous **Windstones** placed at intervals, each inscribed with rune-circles that stabilized local weather and prevented drought. They also shaped the **Varkhon Rise** to channel seasonal winds southward, creating the **Wind Road** -- an invisible highway felt rather than seen. The crescent forests that dot the steppe were planted by Giant hands, and the **Teshkar Vale** was carved by Giant earth-shaping.

#### The Eke Concordant and the Youngest Brother's Children

The **Mytrone** were the last people to join the Concordant and the most reluctant -- already a semi-nomadic culture governed by consensus around campfires. The compromise: they agreed to maintain **Tavranek**, built around the largest Windstone, as their contribution to the administrative network. In exchange, the Concordant recognized their right to self-governance through roaming clans and seasonal gatherings. The Pasua Elves brokered the arrangement.

During the Concordant era, Tavranek grew into a genuine wagon-wheel city linking the Infinite Desert's caravan routes to the southeastern forests.

#### The Shattering of Man

The Mytrone felt the Shattering as vindication rather than catastrophe -- they had always argued that centralized governance was fragile. But the trade routes dried up, the Visetri vitsas drifted away, the Pasua withdrew, and Tavranek shrank. The Mytrone adapted by developing the system of seven semi-permanent camps that radiates from Tavranek.

#### The Deceivers' War and the Running War

When the Deceiver host arrived through the Silence Breach, their full army marched north through the steppe. But the desert broke the invaders' confidence, and a full third of the host reversed course -- back south through the **Gate of Fire** and onto the Eternal Steppe.

The Boss of Tavranek called the seven Headmen to council, and the **Brotherhood of the Steppe** was assembled -- the combined force of **Far Riders** (Mytroan lancers) and **Wind-Riders** (Pasua mounted archers). Together they were the finest light cavalry Oreka had ever seen. The Brotherhood intercepted the returning Lost Third at the Gate of Fire and began the **Running War** -- a nine-day campaign across the open grasslands.

The **Gilded Standard Company** defected to the Kin side during the Running War. The **Battle of Dark Dawn**, on the ninth night, was the climax. The Brotherhood held the line at catastrophic cost -- only one warrior in ten survived the combined campaign.

#### The Brotherhood of the Steppe

The Brotherhood did not begin with the war. After the Shattering, the Pasua retreated to the Gatefall Region. When banditry became a problem, outriders from the Mytrone met with guardian Pasua, and the two groups built the Brotherhood together. By the time the Deceivers came, the Brotherhood had been the steppe's guardian force for a generation.

After Dark Dawn, the battlefield covers roughly forty square miles. Kin-sense flickers unpredictably across it (Will DC 16), and the central area is a dead zone. The Mytrone do not camp on the battlefield. They ride through it quickly, in daylight, and they do not look back.

#### The Present Day

Today the Eternal Steppe is the heart of Mytroan civilization and the largest single-culture region in Oreka. **Boss Sarava Windmane** holds authority by acclamation, replaceable by council at any time. Regional population: approximately 25,000, with a third on the move at any given time.

The **Far Riders** remain the steppe's military elite. The steppe's greatest strength and vulnerability are the same thing: openness.

### Eternal Steppe Landscape and Named Landmarks

The Eternal Steppe occupies the south-central band of Oreka. The terrain is overwhelmingly grassland. Climate is temperate to warm (65-90 degrees F), dry in summer, with brief violent thunderstorms in spring and autumn.

#### The Wind Road

Not a physical road but a corridor of prevailing wind channeled by Giant-shaped terrain. The Mytrone navigate by the Wind Road the way sailors navigate by current. When the Wind Road dies (Survival DC 18), something is wrong: Flamewarg ambush, dead-Kin-sense zone, or incoming storm. Mytroan children learn to read the Wind Road before they learn to read words.

#### The Varkhon Rise

Long, low hills running north-south through the steppe's western quarter, shaped by Giants to channel wind. The steppe's spine -- Mytroan camps pitch on its leeward side, and hilltops are dotted with Windstones.

#### Teshkar Vale

Sheltered depression north of Tavranek with the steppe's only reliable year-round spring. Sacred to the Mytrone -- site of the annual **Wind Gathering**. Non-Mytrone are welcome at the Gathering but not at the rune-renewal ceremony.

#### The Dark Dawn Battlefield

Vast stretch of scorched earth southeast of Tavranek. Burn-scars still visible. Kin-sense flickers (Will DC 16), central area is a dead zone. Herdsmen report horses shying at nothing and a smell of hot pitch on windless summer nights.

#### The Crescent Forests

Giant-era planted forests in crescent/arc patterns anchoring soil and breaking wind. The largest, **Shieldwood**, curves around the southwestern edge. Pasua Elf communities maintain a presence. Interiors are dense enough for concealment -- prime hiding ground for Flamewarg cults.

#### The Windstones

Giant-era standing stones, six to twelve feet tall, pale granite veined with quartz. Approximately forty known. Rune-circles pulse faintly amber on windy nights. All seven semi-permanent camps are sited near Windstones.

#### Dalan's Hold

Fortified waystation on the steppe's southern edge, named for **Dalan Oathspur**, a lancer who held the position alone for three days during the Scattering. Fewer than 300 permanent residents but strategically critical.

## The Infinite Desert

**Region 4 -- The Central Band of Oreka**
*A Regional Guide for Oreka -- v4 -- D&D 3.5e*

> "In the deep sands on thrones left by giants, in cities that are sun-covered paradise -- these oases would inspire the Kin to strive for perfection."

### Infinite Desert History

The **Infinite Desert** is the oldest civilized land in all of Oreka. Long before the Elves wove their first wind-silk or the Dwarves struck their first anvil, the Giants raised their greatest cities here -- along the equatorial belt where the Lord of Stone's influence ran deepest. Their oasis-cities were watered by underground channels and stabilized by rune-circles. Every wall was a relief mural, every bridge an instrument of elemental harmony.

In the age of the **Eke Concordant**, the Giants' final gift was to raise Humanity as their successors. Oreka saw four Keepers in all: the Pre-Keeper, the First and Second Crowned Keepers who ruled wisely, and the Third Crowned Keeper who proved the weakest. During the wise Keepers' age, the Infinite Desert was the administrative heart of all Oreka. Pekakarlik Dwarves and Kovaka Elves maintained the oases. The Na'wasua worked with the Taraf-Imro to raise observatories. Roads connected the Pasua's southern Forest Home with the City of Bridges in the north.

But the Third Keeper was too feeble to hold his realm. The **Shattering of Man** fractured the Concordant beyond repair. The oasis thrones were abandoned. Sand swallowed palaces. The rune-circles began to fail one by one.

Centuries later, the Deceivers marched north into the Infinite Desert. As weeks passed without battle or end to the sands, one sub-commander broke away with a third of the army. The remaining Deceivers pressed north to devastate the Tidebloom and Kinsweave, leaving glass-fused craters in the sand.

> **DM NOTE:** The Lost Third is resolved history. The Far Riders intercepted the Lost Third on the Eternal Steppe in the Running War. Captain Ralven Duskharrow defected and joined the Far Riders. Their combined forces broke the Lost Third at the Battle of Dark Dawn. Northern scholars never learned the full story -- the Far Riders remember through Pasua song and Mytroan oral tradition, not written records.

Today the Infinite Desert is a land of pilgrimage, peril, and profound silence. The Pekakarlik maintain the oldest oases. Kovaka Elves share stewardship of functioning Giant cities. Rarozhki Dwarves hold the volcanic mountains. Halflings tend the oasis groves and run caravan routes. And everywhere, **Sand Wraiths** drift.

> **DM NOTE:** Sand Wraiths are Kin spirits -- born from Kin who died in great anguish or violence. They are NOT Giant spirits. The scholarly debate is player-facing misdirection.

### Infinite Desert Landscape and Named Landmarks

The Infinite Desert wraps around Oreka's equator, functionally separating the northern and southern tiers of civilization. The terrain shifts from towering dune-seas in the deep interior to rocky hardpan near the mountain flanks.

#### The Great River Gorge

Oreka's lifeblood flows from the Giant's Teeth through the desert to the Kailian Sea. In the desert stretch, it has carved a deep canyon whose walls host Pekakarlik cliffside settlements -- tunnel-networked, rune-anchored warrens with boat-docks built into the cliff faces.

#### The Sea Falls

Dramatic cascading waterfalls connecting the Kailian Sea to the Apelian Sea. Halflings and Pekakarlik navigate these treacherous waters (Profession [Sailor] DC 18). The portage routes around them are some of the most profitable trade corridors in the world.

#### The Scorchspires, Emberfangs, and Ashen Crown

Volcanic mountains flanking the desert's edges, home to the Rarozhki Dwarves. Greatest hold: **Kharazhad**, a fortress-forge built into the caldera of a semi-active volcano where the central forge has burned for over three thousand years.

#### The Oasis Ring

Ancient Giant oasis-cities watered by underground channels and stabilized by rune-circles. Pekakarlik Dwarves maintain hydraulic systems; Kovaka Elves tend stone structures and rune-circles. **Sand Warden** pilgrims walk circuits between oases.

#### The Glass Wastes

**4. Fire and Water.** Kharazhad's magma vent is erratic. A Giant-era stabilizing rune requires both fire and water magic -- meaning a Pekakarlik watersmith must enter the Rarozhki's most sacred space. (Diplomacy DC 22.)

### Infinite Desert Campaign Integration

The Infinite Desert is both barrier and bridge. The desert's greatest narrative strength is isolation -- water matters, navigation matters, and the distance between settlements means help is never close.

## The Tidebloom

**Where the Sea Feeds the Kin**
*A Regional Guide for Oreka -- D&D 3.5e Campaign Setting -- v4*

### Tidebloom History

#### The Giant's Legacy and the Kailian Sea

The northeast of Oreka was shaped by the **Kailian Sea** -- the great northern inland freshwater mega-lake. Unlike an ocean, the Kailian has no salt, no true tides. Its water level shifts with seasonal glacial melt, creating a half-year cycle of high water and low water that the Kin turned into Oreka's most remarkable agricultural system. The **Whispering Tidewoods** grew thick and humid in the shelter of the mountains. To the south and west, the **Stonewind Plains** stretched as warm-breezy savannah.

#### The Eke Concordant and the Mithril Chains

The Eke Concordant inherited the Giants' greatest engineering marvel in the northeast: the **Mithril Chains**. Forged during the Age of Giants at the World Forge, these enormous rune-anchored chains stretched into the Kailian Sea, anchoring barges that could brave high-water storms. During high tide, the barges served as floating markets. When waters receded, the barges became planting platforms. Visetri Dwarves and Eruskan Humans worked the tidal fields together, aided by Hasura Elves and Eruskan sorcerers. At **Tide Turn**, the Kin harvested and left plant stalks as tributes to the Elemental Lords. This made Tidebloom Reach one of Oreka's two greatest breadbaskets.

The Concordant era saw nine settlements flourish around the Kailian.

#### The Shattering of Man

Human communities fractured along ethnic lines. Yet Tidebloom suffered less than the Kinsweave -- the Mithril Chains were too valuable to destroy, and the tidal harvest too essential to abandon. Necessity forced fragile cooperation.

> **DM NOTE:** The Tidebloom account of the Shattering is a local folk tradition. The Canon Bible records that the last Keeper was too weak to hold his realm. Tidebloom's version reflects a regional perspective shaped by distance and oral tradition.

#### The Deceivers' War and the Burning

When the Deceivers struck the eastern settlements, four cities burned:

- **Hillmeet** -- sky-oak haven of Hasura Elves, set ablaze by Domnathar sorcery. Lord Pryce Skysong had refused to send aid, declaring the war "not a battle we needs to see." Bard **Aerith Windweave** fell defending the last platform.
- **Foxfen** -- tranquil Hasura grove of awakened trees, torched by ogres. Lord Verith would defend only his own walls. Druid **Sylvara Greenbough** saved a handful before the flames consumed her.
- **Whistlebank** -- thriving Eruskan port, harbor silted by Domnathar magic. Lord Alprenter dismissed the threat as "unlikely." Captain **Marin Wavecrest** died covering the evacuation.
- **Mossgrove** -- drowned under unnatural tides. Lord Rakahda questioned but did nothing. Sorcerer **Mira Seadancer** died guiding the last barges to safety.

The pattern was identical: a petty king who refused to unite, a desperate last stand by heroes, and a city reduced to haunted ruins.

#### The Present Day

Five living cities ring the Kailian Sea: **Velathenor**, **Branmill Cove**, **Tiravel**, **Myruvane**, and **Darnavar**. The Mithril Chains still function. The four burned cities -- collectively the **Ashen Hollows** -- remain abandoned, haunted by Sand Wraiths and Storm Wisps.

The **Ascended Gods** are now worshipped throughout Tidebloom. **Kaile'a, Mistress of Waves** is most beloved. **Cinvarin, the Five Witnesses** is honored as a symbol of unity. **Harreem, the Golden Rose** is whispered about more than openly worshipped. **Semyon the Hearth Mother** is beloved among Halflings. In the far north lies the **Tomb of Kings**, one of seven great Giant burial sites.

Total population: ~28,200. No single ruler.

### Tidebloom Landscape and Named Landmarks

#### The Kailian Sea

Colossal northern freshwater mega-lake with stony cliffs, seasonal storms, and a half-year tidal cycle. The Mithril Chains extend from multiple shoreline points into the shallows. Connected to the southern Apelian Sea by the Sea Falls.

#### Whispering Tidewoods

Vibrant, misty forest along the northeastern shores. Humid and mild (65-75 degrees F). Canopy dominated by sky-oaks hosting Hasura living platforms. Bears scars of the Deceivers' War.

#### Ashen Hollows

The haunted northeastern corner. Heavy mist, chilled breezes (60-65 degrees F). Plagued by Sand Wraiths (CR 5) and Storm Wisps (CR 2). Air tastes of ash even decades later. Charred sky-oak stumps stand like blackened fingers.

#### Tomb of Kings

Colossal Giant burial site in the far north, near Hillmeet's ruins. One of seven such tombs. Massive stone chambers extending deep underground. Tiravel's Hasura treat it as sacred. Reports of ancient guardian constructs and rune-bound elementals still functioning.

#### Stonewind Plains

Vast grasslands between the Tidewoods and the Giant's Teeth. Transitional savannah (70-80 degrees F). Hosts trade routes connecting Tidebloom to Kinsweave.

#### Sundrift Veil

Thinner, sunlit woods south of the Kailian Sea (75-80 degrees F). Less dense and more enchanted. Ideal for small settlements and overland trade.

#### Northfade Veil

Tapering woods north of the Kailian Sea (68-73 degrees F). Less haunted than the Ashen Hollows. Rich with subtle elemental magic.

## Races of Oreka

**OREKA -- Echoes of the Elemental Dawn**
*Complete Reference Compendium*
*Races and Cultures -- Prestige Classes -- Spell Lists*

The **Kin** are the mortal peoples of Oreka, created by the Elemental Lords and shaped by the Giants and Elves during the earliest ages of the world. Their origins follow a divine sequence, each race inheriting a fragment of the elemental powers that forged creation itself.

### The Order of Creation

The Elemental Lords shaped the Kin in a deliberate sequence:

1. **The Firstborn: Giants.** Stewards of creation, builders of the primordial cities, teachers of craft, law, and magic. They harmonized the four elements and raised the first civilizations. Their era ended in a slow, stony fade.
2. **The Secondborn: Elves.** Shaped from divine breath and stone by the Youngest Brother, the Wind Lord. Born to be keepers of art, music, poetry, and dance; became guardians of wisdom and natural harmony.
3. **The Thirdborn: Dwarves.** Carved from mountains and volcanic fire by the Lady of Fire in a moment of solitude and yearning. Enduring craftsmen and defenders of civilization's foundations.
4. **The Fourthborn: Halflings.** Crafted by the Lady of the Sea from seafoam and sand in her playful yearning for voices upon her waters. Small, resilient, and irrepressibly joyful.
5. **The Fifthborn: Humans.** Shaped from all four elements and gifted with adaptability above all other traits. The youngest and most numerous of the Kin.

### Giants -- The Firstborn

> "Enduring titans sculpted from stone and elemental breath, guardians of the world's first dawn."

*Note: Giants are an extinct race and are not available as player characters.*

#### Origin and Mythology

The Giants were created directly by the Elemental Lords -- the only race to bear the mark of every Lord equally. The Lord of Stone provided their bones, the Lady of Fire their molten heart, the Lady of the Sea gave flowing memory and patience, and the Youngest Brother breathed life into their lungs. They were stewards of creation: builders of primordial cities, teachers, and harmonizers of the four elements.

#### History and The Fading

The Giants' greatest tragedy was built into their design. They were immortal but could not reproduce. For millennia this was not a crisis. But eventually the oldest began to fail -- their bodies growing brittle, stone-skin cracking. One by one they crumbled to dust in a process the Elves called **Thalorien** -- the "stone's sleep." There was no disease, no war, no cataclysm.

#### Society and Culture

Giant society was stoic, communal, and focused on stewardship. Art and architecture were inseparable. Governance was council-based, decisions reached through deliberation that could last decades. Their ruins now fill the Infinite Desert, haunted by Sand Wraiths.

#### Appearance

Giants stood 20 to 30 feet tall with stone-textured skin ranging from pale gray to deep earthy brown. Eyes glowed with elemental hues: amber (fire), blue (water), green (earth), pale silver (air). Clothing blended Greek flowing robes with Assyrian geometric patterns.

### Elves -- The Secondborn

> "Children of the Wind Lord, shaped from divine breath and enduring stone."

Elves are the Secondborn Kin, shaped by the Youngest Brother from divine breath and stone. This dual origin -- air and earth -- defines them: creatures of paradox, ephemeral yet ancient, whimsical yet deeply rooted.

Four major subraces emerged: **Hasura** (artists and lightweavers), **Pasua** (storytellers and charmers), **Na'wasua** (enigmatic stargazers), and **Kovaka** (stonebound warriors).

#### General Elven Physiology

All elves stand 5 to 6.5 feet tall with slender builds. They live 300 to 700 years depending on subrace. They do not sleep but enter a 4-hour meditative trance called **elarion** ("wind's rest"), granting immunity to magical sleep. All possess Low-Light Vision, +2 to Listen and Search, and proficiency with longsword, rapier, longbow, and shortbow.

> "Adaptable heirs of a Giant's strength and an Elf's spark, fractured by their own potential."

Created not by an Elemental Lord but by two mortal beings: **Tharok**, the last Giant, and **Lirien**, the first Elven matriarch. Tharok grounded them with endurance and strength; Lirien contributed adaptability and creative spark. **Aeloria** (Lirien's sister) ensured viability by weaving all four elements into a flexible framework. Four ethnic lines were launched: **Eruskan** (water), **Mytroan** (wind), **Taraf-Imro** (fire), and **Orean** (earth).

Tharok poured his remaining vital energy into the project and quietly crumbled to dust. Lirien raised a memorial pillar that still stands today. The **Shattering of Man** later fractured humanity when Taraf-Imro chieftain **Varkis** obtained forbidden Deceiver technology and attempted to seize the Great River by force.

All humans carry traces of all four elements, receive a bonus feat at 1st level, and live 70 to 100 years.

#### Eruskan Humans

> "Fluid mariners guided by the sea's rhythm."

Water-children. Navigators, traders, diplomats. Sea-blue eyes, dark hair, weathered features. +2 Int, +1 Dex, +1 Wis. Spell-like: 1/day create water. Water's Grace (Ex): +2 Swim (stacks), +1 dodge AC in/on water. Bonus feat at 1st level. Favored Class: Any (Rogue/Sorcerer common).

#### Mytroan Humans

> "Free spirits riding the plains' wild winds."

Wind-children. Horsemen, scouts, storytellers. Pale blond or ash-brown hair, stormy gray eyes, tall wiry build. +2 Dex, +1 Wis, +1 Cha. Spell-like: 1/day feather fall. Windrider's Grace (Ex): +2 Ride (stacks), +1 Reflex in open terrain. Bonus feat. Favored Class: Any (Ranger/Barbarian common).

#### Taraf-Imro Humans

> "Fierce warriors tempered by volcanic flame."

Fire-children. Warriors, smiths, fire mages. Fiery red hair, amber eyes, stocky powerful build. +2 Str, +1 Con, +1 Cha. Spell-like: 1/day produce flame. Resistance 5 to fire. Fire's Fury (Ex): +2 fire damage, +1 vs. fire effects. Bonus feat. All martial weapons, medium armor. Favored Class: Any (Fighter/Sorcerer common).

#### Orean Humans

> "Steadfast builders rooted in the mountain's strength."

Earth-children. Farmers, builders, stone-carvers. Dark hair, earthy brown eyes, broad sturdy build. +2 Con, +1 Str, +1 Wis. Spell-like: 1/day stone shape. Earth's Stability (Ex): +2 vs. earth effects, +1 CMD vs. bull rush. Bonus feat. Favored Class: Any (Fighter/Druid common).

### Hybrid Peoples

#### Half-Giants

> "Titanic heirs bridging ancient might and mortal will."

Emerged from rare unions between fading Giants and early Humans. Stand 8 to 12 feet tall with stone-textured skin (granite gray, sandstone gold, or basalt black). They carry the All-Element Concord of Giants in diminished form.

**Racial Traits:** Large size. +4 Str, +2 Con, -2 Dex, -2 Cha. Low-Light Vision. Spell-like: 1/day stone shape or produce flame (chosen each day). Resistance 5 to fire. Earth's Endurance (Ex): 50% falling damage reduction, +2 vs. exhaustion/fatigue. Powerful Build (Ex): wield weapons one size larger, treated as Huge for grapple/bull rush/trip. Favored Class: Fighter or Cleric. **LA +1**. Requires DM approval.

#### Silentborn

> "The children of stolen ancestry -- echoes of forbidden craft."

The most tragic race in Oreka. Hybrids of Kin and Domnathar, created through brutal breeding experiments -- meant to be weapons, not a people. Their Kin-sense registers as **null resonance** -- a silence where the Song of the World should be.

The **Silent Concord** is organized into **Choirs**: Veil (counter-infiltration), Hearth (hidden refuges), Warden (combat), Memory (lore), and Dawn (diplomacy). Core laws: No Binding, No Chains. No Birth Without Choice. Nothing Done Alone.

**Racial Traits:** +2 Wis, +2 Dex, -2 Cha. Darkvision 30 ft. Spell-like: 1/day detect thoughts, 1/day shadow veil (invisibility in dim light). Silent Echo (Su): 1/day +4 insight on Spot/Listen/Sense Motive. Unnatural Resilience (Ex): +2 vs. charm/mind-affecting. Residual Kin-Sense (Su): +4 to detect deception from Kin or Domnathar blood. Null Resonance (Ex): invisible on Kin-sense. Light Sensitivity. Favored Class: Rogue or Ranger. **LA +0**.

#### Half-Domnathar

> "Children of two worlds -- neither welcomed, nor fully rejected."

Arise from Kin and Deceiver bloodlines through infiltration, forced unions, or (rarely) genuine relationships. Register on Kin-sense as weak, discordant Kin -- a cracked resonance that almost resolves but never quite does.

**Racial Traits:** +2 Dex, -2 Cha. Darkvision 60 ft. Mixed Blood (Ex): immune to magical sleep, +2 vs. enchantment. Shadow Drills (Ex): +2 Hide, Move Silently, Intimidate. Light Sensitivity. Elf-Blooded Hybrid (Su): counts as Kin for harmful effects, DM may also treat as Domnathar. Favored Class: Rogue or Fighter. **LA +0**.

### Other Peoples and Non-Kin Races

On Kin-sense, these peoples register as **wild static** -- present, alive, but fundamentally different from elemental resonance.

#### Farborn

> "Strangers who chose honor over obedience, and built a home from nothing."

Descendants of the Gilded Standard Company. Non-Kin humans from another world. Population ~28,000 at Titan's Rest. Their **Titanbreed** horses are the most coveted cavalry mounts on the continent.

Two cavalry traditions: **Wind-Riders** (light/medium, Titanbreed hybrids, composite longbows and sabers) and **Titan Knights** (heavy cavalry, full plate on Titanbreed destriers).

Motto: "Your life is worth more than your master."

**Racial Traits:** Standard Human (+2 to one ability score). Bonus feat, bonus skills. Cavalry Training (Ex): lance, composite longbow, and saber as martial; +1 attack when mounted. Non-Kin (Ex): invisible on Kin-sense, immune to Kin-targeting effects. Favored Class: Fighter or Ranger. **LA +0**.

#### Hobgoblins

> "Disciplined soldiers forged in chains, now fighting for masters or for themselves."

The Deceivers' professional soldiers. Most remain with Deceiver remnants; a minority have broken free as **Iron Tribes**. Their **Iron Law** code predates arrival in Oreka.

**Racial Traits:** +2 Dex, +2 Con. Darkvision 60 ft. +4 Move Silently. Battle Hardened (Ex): +2 vs. fear. Command (Ex): adjacent hobgoblin allies gain +1 attack and +1 vs. fear. Non-Kin. All simple/martial weapons, light/medium armor, shields. Favored Class: Fighter. **LA +1**.

#### Wargs

> "Intelligent wolf-like warriors whose loyalty must be earned, never commanded."

Fully sapient beings who were catastrophically underestimated by their Domnathar masters. During the **Great Howl of Freedom** at Dark Dawn, thousands broke their chains. **Wargbond Tribes** are genuinely egalitarian goblin-warg partnerships.

**Racial Traits:** +2 Str, +2 Wis, -2 Cha. Speed 50 ft. Low-Light Vision, Scent. Bite 1d6 + Str. Quadruped (Ex): +4 vs. bull rush/trip, x1.5 carrying, cannot use hand items. Elemental Sensitivity (Su): detect Kin-sense resonance as wild static. Non-Kin. Favored Class: Ranger or Druid. **LA +1**.

#### Goblins

> "Cunning survivors shaped by oppression and sudden freedom."

The Deceivers' servitor race -- expendable infantry, tunnel engineers, trap-builders. During Dark Dawn, tens of thousands seized their freedom. Modern societies: **Free Tribes**, **Hill Tribes**, **Wargbond Tribes**, and **Tinker Clans**.

**Racial Traits:** Small. +2 Dex, -2 Str, -2 Cha. Darkvision 60 ft. +4 Move Silently, +4 Ride. Light Sensitivity. Non-Kin. Favored Class: Rogue. **LA +0**.

#### Tanuki

> "Forest tricksters with a gift for transformation and joy."

Native to the Great Forests, created by forest spirits. Kin-adjacent -- recognized as natural but not Kin. Raccoon-like features, 3-4 ft tall, minor shapeshifting.

**Racial Traits:** Small. Fey type. +2 Cha, +2 Dex, -2 Str. Low-Light Vision. Spell-like: 1/day disguise self. Spirit Brew (Su): 1 dose/day, DC 12 Will or answer one question truthfully. Kin-Adjacent (Ex): faint warm static on Kin-sense. Favored Class: Bard or Druid. **LA +0**.

#### River Serpents

Sacred wardens of the Great River, created during the Giants' era. Large serpentine beings with riverstone scales. Not fully sapient but possess deep, ancient intelligence responding to elemental resonance and emotional states. Pekakarlik Dwarves consider them holy.

#### Storm Wisps

Elemental spirits of limited sapience appearing in treetops during storms -- crackling spheres of blue-white light. Curious, reactive, unpredictable. Electric touch (CR 2, 1d4 damage). Mytroan nomads leave tent-flaps open during storms to welcome them.

#### Sand Wraiths

> "The desert's mourning shadows -- echoes of the Giants' passing."

Undead-elemental fusions born from spirits of Kin who died in great anguish. Towering humanoid shapes of swirling sand and dust with dim amber eyes. They warn trespassers, guard sacred ruins, and represent the sorrow of the past. CR 5, desiccating touch.

### Kin-Sense, Elemental Resonance and Reproductive Rules

#### Kin-Sense

A primal, elemental resonance shared by all true Kin. Not a spell, not a skill -- it is ontological. It functions as a subconscious awareness of belonging.

**The Resonance Taxonomy:**

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Harmonic (Kin)** | Warm, sympathetic vibration. Feels natural and right. All true Kin register this way. |
| **Absolute Silence (Void)** | Complete absence. A hole in the world. All Domnathar register this way. Cannot be hidden or disguised. |
| **Null Resonance (Silent)** | Absence where resonance should exist -- a gap in the melody. Silentborn, Severed, Oath-Broken. |
| **Wild Static (Non-Kin)** | Present and alive but fundamentally different. Farborn, Hobgoblins, Goblins, Wargs. Does not trigger unease. |
| **Warm Static (Kin-Adjacent)** | Faint, pleasant variant. Suggests kinship without full resonance. Tanuki and certain forest spirits. |

**Mechanics:**
- **Range:** 60 ft normal; 30 ft in elemental disturbance; 120 ft in deep calm. Passes through 5 ft of solid barriers. Blocked by lead, worked Domnathar metal, or False Silence.
- **Automatic Detection:** Within range, presence and general category are detected automatically (no action).
- **Active Concentration:** Move action to focus on a specific individual, revealing confirmed category, approximate strength, and whether stable or distressed.
- **Vs. Deception:** Mundane disguises do not fool Kin-sense. Magical disguises create temporary false resonance (Kin-sense check vs. spell DC to detect discrepancy).
- **Vs. Silence and Void:** No check required. Any Kin character within range automatically perceives the absence.

**Edge Cases:**
- **Crowds (20+):** DC 15 + 1 per 10 additional people to find Void/Null in a crowd.
- **Antimagic:** Suppresses Kin-sense (it is supernatural).
- **Undead:** Simply absent, like furniture. Distinct from Void. Ghosts may retain faint flickering echo (DC 20).
- **Constructs:** No registration. Pure elementals register as overwhelming raw static (DC 15 Will or dazzled 1 round within 10 ft).
- **Dying Kin:** Resonance flares (detectable at twice normal range) then collapses -- felt as a sharp, painful loss.

#### Elemental Resonance

The four elements -- Stone, Fire, Water, Wind -- are the substance of creation. Every Kin race carries an elemental attunement:

- **Giants / Half-Giants:** All four elements in equilibrium (All-Element Concord)
- **Elves:** Primary Wind/Air; secondary varies by subrace
- **Dwarves:** Primary Earth and Fire; secondary varies by subrace
- **Halflings:** Primary Water; secondary Life/Growth
- **Humans:** Mixed all elements with cultural variation (Orean=Earth, Eruskan=Water, Mytroan=Wind, Taraf-Imro=Fire)

**Elemental Tolerance:** All Kin gain Resistance 2 against their primary element's energy type. Giants/Half-Giants gain Resistance 2 to all four.

**In Magic:** +1 caster level for spells aligned with primary element (duration/range/effect, not damage/DCs). -1 caster level for opposed element (Earth/Wind opposed; Fire/Water opposed). Penalty negatable by Elemental Versatility feat or full-round casting.

#### Reproductive Rules

**Core Principles:**
1. **Elemental Compatibility.** Kin can reproduce with other Kin because they share the same four-element matrix.
2. **Resonance Determines Kinship.** Both Kin parents = always Kin child. Kin + Non-Kin = 50/50 Kin or Non-Kin. Kin + Domnathar = almost always Silentborn.
3. **Physical Form Follows the Mother.** Cross-race children's physical form favors the mother's race.

**Cross-Race Compatibility:**

| Pairing | Result |
|---|---|
| Kin + Kin (same race) | Always fertile. Normal child. |
| Kin + Kin (different race) | Fertile in most combinations. Physical form favors mother. Mixed-race Kin are fully accepted. |
| Kin + Non-Kin (Farborn, etc.) | Fertile. 50% Kin (diminished resonance) / 50% Non-Kin (wild static). |
| Kin + Domnathar (Void) | Fertile only through magical intervention. Child almost always Silentborn. ~1 in 100 produces Half-Domnathar. |
| Kin + Undead/Construct | Not fertile under any known circumstances. |
| Non-Kin + Non-Kin | Fertile within same race. Standard D&D rules for cross-species. |
| Kin-Adjacent + Kin | Not normally fertile. Exceptions possible through powerful fey magic (undocumented). |

**Silentborn Edge Cases:**
- Silentborn + Kin = ~60% Kin child, ~40% null resonance or wild static
- Silentborn + Silentborn = ~80% null resonance child
- Can a Silentborn become True Kin? The Domnathar say no. The Silent Concord says maybe. No confirmed case. The possibility terrifies the Domnathar.
- **Severed Kin** can potentially be restored through the **Reharmonize** rite (Chord Circle, 4 Kin of all elements, base 50% success, modified by Wisdom and time since Severing).
