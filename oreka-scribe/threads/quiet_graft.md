## Summary

Vaerix Caelnorath's forty-year project to breed a Domnathar-Farborn hybrid heir capable of interfacing with Kin shrine infrastructure. The player — a Kin-sense-capable scholar arriving at Spur Tower — is the asymmetric observer who gradually uncovers the project, its principals, and its cost.

Four acts. Twenty-five sessions. The player's choices determine whether this ends as tragedy (Vaerix's fall), eucatastrophe (someone is saved at the last moment), or loss-of-numinous (the project succeeds but the world is less for it).

## The Seven Principals

1. **Vaerix** — the architect. Knows everything. Controls nothing that matters. Never in the room. Works through layers of Orekan staff and Unstrung contacts. The player may not know he exists until Act 3.
2. **Sarre** — the vessel. Knows he's part of a theory. Doesn't know he's a tool.
3. **Maeren** — the variable. Knows she chose exile. Doesn't know she was chosen first.
4. **Khadi** — the file. Knows something is wrong. Doesn't know what it's for.
5. **Griff** — the search. Knows his daughter is gone. Doesn't know where or why. May have sent the player.
6. **Michele** — the placement. Unstrung operative who curated the player's posting on Vaerix's behalf. The player doesn't know Michele exists.
7. **Heln** — the face. Halfling archivist who runs Spur Tower's front-facing operations. Takes instructions from a desk she's never seen occupied. The player's primary contact.

The player is the eighth — the only one who can see across all seven, because their Kin-sense works where the Domnathar's doesn't. But they were placed here by two different patrons, neither of whom knows about the other.

## Open Questions

- What does the player do when they understand what Vaerix is building?
- Can Maeren be warned without destroying her marriage?
- Will Khadi report the file, or act on it herself?
- When does the father arrive? What does he do when he finds out?
- Is the child the solution Vaerix thinks it is, or something else entirely?
- Does Sarre's love survive learning the truth about his role?

## Act Structure (Proposed)

**Act 1 — The Stacks (sessions 1-6):** Player arrives at Spur Tower for the feat. Meets Heln (the archivist — helpful, professional, slightly nervous about the restricted section). Sent to Thornbend for the lodestone. Overhears Griff and Michele's names at the taproom. Earns the feat. Begins research. Encounters Sarre (friendly scholar in the envoy quarter) and Maeren (guarded Farborn woman who shouldn't be here). Vaerix is invisible — just a signature on approval notes. Kin-sense shows the player things no one else in the tower can see.

**Act 2 — The Road (sessions 7-12):** Player follows the threads outside the tower. Griff (if contacted — the father, the grief, the cold patience). Dry Fork (Khadi, the weigh-master, the file she's holding). Michele (a name that keeps appearing at the edges). The player's research gives them legitimate reasons to visit these places. They start connecting the inquiries. Eleven files. Two cutouts. Someone was shopping for Farborn. The player begins to understand the shape but not the architect.

**Act 3 — The Architect (sessions 13-18):** The trail leads back to Spur Tower. The notes on Heln's desk. The forty-year filing pattern. The restricted section the player is now inside. Sarre's innocence becomes visible — he loves Maeren, he doesn't know why Vaerix brought them together. Maeren's shrine-touch surfaces. The player realizes the architect is Domnathar, is inside the tower, has been watching them through the filing system since session 1. **First confrontation with Vaerix** — not a greeting. A reckoning. The Domnathar steps out from behind his infrastructure and the player sees what they've been standing inside.

**Act 4 — The Reckoning (sessions 19-25):** Griff is arriving. Khadi acts or is acted upon. Vaerix's project reaches its critical moment — Maeren may be pregnant, or the shrine-touch has been tested again, or Sarre has learned the truth. The player — now aware of both patrons, the architect, the variables, and their own instrumentalization — must choose. Multiple resolution paths. Vaerix is a potential boss fight if the player confronts him directly. The Domnathar fights when there's nothing left to delegate.
