## Resolution: Option C — Both Patrons

Griff sent the player. Vaerix — through Michele — intercepted the arrangement, amplified it, made sure the specific scholar Griff found was the one with the right Kin-sense profile, and ensured the posting went through smoothly. The player was sent by Griff AND placed by Vaerix, and neither patron knows about the other.

## What the Player Knows at Start

"A Farborn caravan-master named Griff is paying my expenses. His daughter disappeared near Domnathar territory. He can't go himself. He asked me to take a research posting at Spur Tower — it was available, the work is real, and while I'm there, keep my eyes open for a Farborn woman who vanished four months ago."

The research contract is genuine. The player is a real scholar who genuinely cares about the academic work. Griff's request is a side job — important but secondary. This is critical: a scholar who's only a spy is boring. A scholar who's a scholar first and a spy by accident is interesting.

What the player does NOT know: Vaerix's cutout network, through Michele, identified Griff's search for a researcher, evaluated the candidates Griff was considering, and nudged the posting board to favor the one with the strongest Kin-sense profile. The research contract the player applied for was already going to happen. Michele just made sure *this* scholar got it. The player earned the posting legitimately — it was just also curated.

## Two Reveals

**Reveal 1 (Act 2, around session 8-10):** The player discovers Griff's side of the story has more weight than expected. Maeren is alive. She's here. She chose to be here. Telling Griff means destroying what she chose. The loyalty the player owes Griff becomes a burden. This is the *visible* moral crisis.

**Reveal 2 (Act 3, around session 15-17):** The player discovers their posting was curated. The research contract was real, but someone made sure *they* got it. The trail leads back through Michele to the same cutout network that studied Maeren. The player realizes: "I was sent by the father AND placed by the architect. I have been both the rescue party and the experiment." This recontextualizes everything they did in Acts 1-2. Every observation they made, every Kin-sense reading — Vaerix was watching.

## What This Does to Each Act

**Act 1 (sessions 1-6):** Player arrives with two jobs — real research and Griff's quiet search. Gets to know Spur Tower. Meets Sarre (friendly), Maeren (guarded), Vaerix (polite). Research is interesting. Griff's job is a slow burn in the background. Kin-sense gives the player information that doesn't quite add up yet.

**Act 2 (sessions 7-12):** Player's research takes them outside Spur Tower. Encounters the trade post network. Dry Fork. Khadi's operation. The father's pattern. Finds Maeren — alive, married, grieving silently. Reveal 1 hits: do you tell Griff? The visible moral crisis consumes the act.

**Act 3 (sessions 13-18):** Player investigates the posting itself (or Michele surfaces, or Khadi's file points backward to the cutout network). Reveal 2 hits: the posting was curated. Everything recontextualizes. Vaerix's patience frays as the player asks questions he didn't anticipate. Sarre's innocence becomes visible. The shrine-touch secret surfaces.

**Act 4 (sessions 19-25):** All threads converge. Griff arrives (or is arriving). Khadi acts (or is acted upon). Vaerix's project reaches its critical moment. The player — now fully aware of both patrons — must choose whose tool to stop being and what to do with what they know. Multiple resolution paths.

## The Core Irony

The player was hired to find a missing woman. She wasn't missing — she was hidden. The player was placed to be an observer. They weren't observing — they were being observed. The hero's journey has a false call (Griff's request) and a manufactured threshold (the curated posting). The real journey begins when the player sees both layers and decides to act on their own judgment — not as Griff's investigator, not as Vaerix's variable, but as themselves.
