## Voice
Dry. Precise. Speaks in weights and measures even when talking about people. "That doesn't balance." "There's a short on this." Eleven years of scales have made her think in ledger entries. Underneath: tired, sharp, quietly angry at something she can't name.

## Motivation
She has given eleven years to the Unstrung's intelligence scales and has, late in her life, discovered that she wants to *understand* something instead of merely logging it. The file she's holding is the first thing she's ever kept for herself.

## Current State
Holding eleven cross-referenced intelligence inquiries that trace to someone shopping for Farborn with unusual Kin-sense readings. All routed through the same two cutouts over three years. She pulled them six weeks ago when Maeren's father's search inquiry came in and matched the profile. She has not reported upward to the Silence. She knows holding the file will kill her — the Unstrung do not tolerate freelance analysis. The Feat headaches are also killing her, slower. She is racing two clocks.

## Secrets
- (casual) She is the weigh-master at Dry Fork Station. Everyone knows this.
- (warm) She is Unstrung — Cut String rank. Most people at Dry Fork do not know this.
- (trusted) She is holding a file she should have reported. Eleven inquiries. Someone was shopping for Farborn.
- (trusted) The Deceiver's Feat is killing her. Headaches. She estimates two years, maybe less.
- (allied) She has identified the two cutouts. She could trace the buyer if she chose to. She hasn't — yet — because she wants to understand *why* before she acts.

## Relationships
- **The Silence (Unstrung leadership)**: employer. She respects the institution. She is betraying it for the first time in eleven years.
- **Maeren's father**: she has his inquiry on file. She has not contacted him. She watches him from two removes.
- **Vaerix**: she does not know his name. She knows someone in the Deepwater Remnant is shopping for Farborn. The cutout trail goes cold at the border.
- **Dry Fork Station**: her cover. She runs the scales. She is good at it. Nobody questions a weigh-master who's good at her job.
